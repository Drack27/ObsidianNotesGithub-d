# The Forgotten

**Type:** Secondary Entity
**Associated Fear:** Athazagoraphobia — the fear of being forgotten, ignored, or replaced.
**Case Connections:** [[The Antimemetic Killer]], [[The Case of the Blurred Woman]]

---

## Nature

The Forgotten is one of the Secondary Entities — lesser, localized fear manifestations born from the mixing of the primary [[The Great Entities|Great Entities]]. It is the supernatural embodiment of the fear of being forgotten: the dread that you could vanish from the world and no one would notice, that your name could be erased and no one would care, that you could simply stop mattering.

Like all Entities, The Forgotten lacks true consciousness, intent, or will. It is more like a hateful color bleeding into reality — a force that concentrates where fear concentrates and manifests when conditions are right. It does not *choose* to do things. It *happens* where the fear of being forgotten is strong enough.

---

## Presence in Roselake

The Forgotten has a growing presence in Roselake, driven by two converging factors:

### The Rising Contrast
The general rise in contrast between the **world's connectedness** and **Roselake's disconnectedness** is driving a number of related fears. The internet, cable news, and the broader culture's accelerating interconnectedness have made Roselake's isolation feel sharper and more absolute than ever. The town was always remote; now it feels *left behind.*

This contrast feeds three related Secondary Entities:
- **The Isolation** — the fear of being cut off.
- **The Unprotected** — the fear of being beyond help.
- **The Forgotten** — the fear of being erased.

The contrast has reached a **tipping point** at which individuals feeling strong fear of being forgotten in the moment create a supernatural "weak spot" — a localized concentration of athazagoraphobia intense enough for The Forgotten's influence to manifest in tangible ways.

### Jonathan Oesch
**[[Jonathan Oesch]]** is a major driver of The Forgotten's presence. Every use of [[The Catalyst]] — the ritual spell granted to him by The Forgotten — strengthens the entity. The spell literally makes forgetting *real*, generating exactly the kind of fear that The Forgotten feeds on. Jonathan believes he is using a tool for his own revenge. In reality, he is doing The Forgotten's work.

## Manifestations

The Forgotten manifests in several ways in Roselake:

### The Catalyst
The ritual spell used by Jonathan Oesch. See [[The Catalyst]] for full details. The Catalyst is the most direct and dramatic manifestation of The Forgotten's influence — a spell that literally erases people from memory.

### The Blurred Woman
**[[The Case of the Blurred Woman|The Blurred Woman]]** is a pre-existing pseudo-avatar of The Forgotten. She was a real woman from the mid-to-late 1800s who was deliberately forgotten by her community. She now exists in a limbo state between the physical and supernatural realms, unable to fully manifest or fully pass on.

The Forgotten's growing presence in Roselake has created more "weak spots" where she can appear. She can only manifest in places and times where the fear of being forgotten is strong — there is plenty of it in Roselake as background radiation, but it requires a higher-than-ambient concentration for her to actually appear. If someone connects their own fear of being forgotten to the woman's blurred face, she could begin to manifest more consistently to that person, developing into a sustained haunting. This has not yet happened.

The Blurred Woman is an **unwilling host** of The Forgotten. She does not want to serve it. She wants to be remembered — which is the opposite of what The Forgotten represents. Her unwillingness is all that keeps her from becoming The Forgotten's vessel and full manifestation on Earth. If she were antagonized sufficiently, she could choose to turn from an unwilling captive to a fully willing Avatar. If this happens, Roselake has much bigger problems.

### Ambient Effects
In areas of concentrated athazagoraphobia, The Forgotten creates subtle ambient effects:
- People become harder to remember after they leave a room.
- Names slip from the tip of the tongue more easily.
- Photographs seem to fade or blur slightly at the edges.
- A pervasive sense of loneliness and insignificance settles over the area.

## Avatars and Agents

### Jonathan Oesch
Jonathan is a **tool** of The Forgotten, but not its vessel. He is barred from becoming a full Avatar because people in his community still know about him — he has not been forgotten. His physical presence and his community's awareness of him (however minimal) anchor him to the living world in a way that prevents The Forgotten from fully claiming him.

### The Blurred Woman
The Blurred Woman is the closest thing The Forgotten has to a potential Avatar in Roselake. Unlike Jonathan, she has no one who remembers her. Her unwillingness to serve is the only barrier. See [[The Case of the Blurred Woman]] for full details.
