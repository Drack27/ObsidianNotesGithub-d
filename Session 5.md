# Session 5

**Date (in-game):** March 17, 1981, morning through late evening
**Player Characters:** [[Matthew Davis]] and [[Reverend Detty|Reverend George "Detty" Ndetti]]
**Players:** Tucker (Matthew) and Colton (Detty)

---

## Events

### Morning at Joe's Superstore

On the third day of his recovery from the [[Anglerfish]] attack, [[Matthew Davis]] woke still in pain but regained a point of health overnight. He and [[Agatha Graves]] had planned to meet up that day for another stakeout at [[Mount Roselake]], but Agatha did not make the rendezvous, and Matthew decided to proceed alone.

Before heading out, he drove to [[Joe's Superstore]] to stock up. The morning store was quieter than he remembered it — less of the idle chatter that had filled the aisles on earlier visits. [[Wendy Simons]] was not on her usual register. In her place was a scruffy, stubble-faced man Matthew had not seen before. Matthew made his purchase — approximately 500 rounds of .45 caliber ammunition, around $100 worth — and returned home to wait out the day.

### Arrival at Mount Roselake

As darkness fell, Matthew drove out to Mount Roselake to resume the stakeout. He reached the base of the hill around 9 PM under the first cloudless sky in days, parked, and began his climb. He attempted to sweep the area for signs of the creature on the way up (Spot Hidden — failed), but spotted nothing.

Independently — and without knowing Matthew would be there — [[Reverend Detty|Reverend George "Detty" Ndetti]], the pastor of [[Reverend Detty|First Methodist]], pulled up to the same dirt parking area in his own vehicle. Detty had seen one of the old "Roselake Ghost Hunters Club" flyers posted around town and grown uneasy about non-clergy-sanctioned supernatural investigation in his community. He had also been concerned about one of his parishioners — [[Wendy Simons]] — who had stopped attending church and begun behaving strangely at the register. He had come up the hill alone to see for himself who was running the ghost club.

The two men found each other at the trailhead. After initial wariness, they exchanged what they knew: Matthew had been attacked by an unknown animal after the first Ghost Hunters meeting on March 14 and was now investigating with Agatha; Detty had been tracking strange behavior in his congregation and was concerned about Wendy Simons specifically. Detty mentioned she hadn't been in church in a week and had been stiff and distant — staring straight ahead — at the store. Matthew confirmed the cashier he'd seen on earlier visits had not been there that morning.

They agreed to investigate together.

### Search of the Radio Station

They made their way up the hill to the [[The Old Radio Tower|Old Radio Tower]]. The front door stood ajar, as it had on Matthew's previous visits. The top floor was as he remembered it — the broken On-Air sign hanging by a wire, the rusted radio equipment, the cobwebs in the corners. Matthew (hard Spot Hidden) confirmed nothing had changed on that level.

The basement was a different story.

Thomas McMullin's desk was still there. The typewriter was still there. The lamp was still on, flickering overhead. The corkboard still hung on the east wall, its red yarn still strung across its face. But every folder Matthew had seen on his earlier visits was gone. The manila folders on the desk were gone. The plastic crate held only empty file folders. The Skinwalkers folder that had sat on the ledge beneath the corkboard was gone. The board itself was stripped of the photographs and index cards that had covered it.

(Canonically, though neither PC knew it: [[Victor Hayes]] had come through earlier the same evening and taken the lot. See [[Session 4]].)

In one corner, the half-rotted cardboard boxes of old recording tapes had been disturbed. One box had been ripped open haphazardly. Inside, water-damaged reel-to-reel tapes were visible — severely moldy, their labels smeared to illegibility, the whole lot smelling musty. Matthew spent luck to confirm (hard Spot Hidden, pushed) that nothing else in the basement had been moved or removed beyond what he could already see.

### The Generator and the Footprints

They exited through the basement's side door and walked around to the rusted generator mounted against the exterior wall. Matthew gave the unit an extreme-success mechanical examination: the generator was non-functional, and somebody had been recently tinkering with it — screws loosened, a wrench left on top. Detty confirmed the read (extreme success on his own). Neither could figure out how the basement lamp was still lit. The power source, whatever it was, was not that generator.

Detty then swept the perimeter for tracks. On a regular Tracking success he found **multiple sets of human shoeprints** around the station, left within the last couple of days. Matthew, trying to get a better read on what Detty had found, pushed his own tracking attempt with archaeological precision. He spent seven luck points trying to succeed — and succeeded only in erasing the prints himself while working the ground.

### The Tower Falls

They turned their attention to the radio tower itself — forty to fifty feet of rusty scaffolding with a single interior ladder behind a guard cage. Metallic creaking and groaning came off it with every gust of wind. They started up.

Partway up, a stronger gust hit and the tower leaned violently. Matthew threw his arm around the scaffolding to hold on. Detty, attempting the same, fumbled his Dexterity roll and slipped.

Matthew fell approximately twenty feet and took **12 damage in a single hit**, dropping him below 0 HP instantly. He landed with a **compound fracture of his right arm** and a **broken spine**. Detty fell too, took 6 damage, and broke his own right arm — a serious major wound, but significantly less severe than Matthew's.

Matthew was unconscious. Detty — his own arm now useless — stabilized him with an excellent First Aid roll, buying time before Matthew's condition could worsen further.

### Retreat to the Fire Station

Detty spent 10 luck points to succeed on a strength check (with a penalty die for his broken arm) to drag the unconscious Matthew down the hill to his own car. The descent took roughly thirty minutes, over uneven ground, with one functioning arm and an unconscious man.

He loaded Matthew into the back seat and drove one-handed into town, arriving at the [[Roselake Fire Station]] around 10:30 to 11 PM.

At the station, the older female dispatcher (matching the curmudgeonly woman [[Matthew Davis|Matthew]] and [[Agatha Graves|Agatha]] had met in [[Session 3]]) took one look at the situation and moved Matthew onto a gurney in the station's makeshift medical room. On an excellent Medicine roll, she reset the compound fracture and realigned Matthew's spine — cigarette clenched between her teeth, muttering about how badly this could have gone. She warned Detty that Matthew's spine had been dangerously misaligned and that he might yet be left permanently paralyzed if he didn't rest. Matthew made a Constitution check against the pain of the reset and, still unconscious, did not thrash or wake.

The on-duty firefighter — a clean-shaven young man, maybe twenty — handled Detty's arm next: splinted it, administered first aid, and expressed real worry about the two of them going back out. "Please don't go up there again," he told the Reverend. "We need you around this town, especially now."

The dispatcher, for her part, was less gentle. She scolded Detty for his "wild theories" and for nearly getting Matthew killed, lit another cigarette indoors, and slammed the door as she left the medical room.

Matthew remained unconscious at 2/11 HP. Detty, conscious but injured, stayed at the fire station to let his arm be set and observed overnight.

## Significance

This session introduced [[Reverend Detty|Reverend George "Detty" Ndetti]] as an active investigator — a [[Reverend Detty|First Methodist]] pastor drawn in by his own parishioners' strange behavior (especially [[Wendy Simons]]'s) and by the Ghost Hunters Club flyer still posted around town. Matthew and Detty met for the first time at Mount Roselake and explored the radio station together.

The basement's state was the session's biggest revelation: **every case folder and all of the Skinwalkers corkboard's contents were missing.** Matthew and Detty interpreted this as evidence that someone else had been through the station within the last few days and cleared the place out — an interpretation consistent with the disturbed boxes of recording tapes, the tampered-with (but non-functional) generator, the mysterious still-working basement light, and the multiple sets of recent human shoeprints around the building. Neither man knows that the files were taken by [[Victor Hayes]] earlier the same evening.

The radio tower itself proved nearly fatal. A gust of wind leaned the structure and both men fell. Matthew was critically injured — compound fracture, broken spine, temporary paralysis. The fire station dispatcher reset him, but her warning stood: he needs around four weeks of bed rest to recover mobility, and if he pushes himself and fails his major-wound recovery, the paralysis could become permanent. Detty's arm was also broken, though his injuries are less severe; he will need time to heal but not under the same condition.

Both investigators are now injured and temporarily out of commission. The files they might have reviewed are in the hands of a third party they don't know about. And [[Wendy Simons]] — whatever is currently wearing her — is still out there.
