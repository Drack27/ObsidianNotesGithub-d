# The Secondary Entities

**Type:** Localized Fear Manifestations
**Origin:** Born from the mixing of the primary [[The Great Entities|Great Entities]]

---

## Nature

The Secondary Entities are lesser, localized fear manifestations that arise when a specific flavor of fear — a particular blend of the primary Great Entities — concentrates in a geographic area intensely enough to take on a life of its own. They are not as powerful as the Great Entities, but they are more *specific*, more *focused*, and in some ways more dangerous because of it. A Great Entity is a diffuse force — a color bleeding into reality. A Secondary Entity is that color concentrated into a single point, burning bright enough to spawn creatures and warp the world around it.

Like the Great Entities, Secondary Entities lack true consciousness, intent, or will. They are forces, not minds. They happen where fear happens, and they grow where fear grows.

---

## Secondary Entities Active in Roselake

### The Isolation
**Associated Fear:** The fear of being cut off — from people, from help, from the world.
**Composition:** A roughly equal mix of all three primary Great Entities: **The Predator** (the animal terror of being alone and vulnerable), **The Inevitable** (the crushing dread that isolation will never end), and **The Uncanny** (the creeping sense that something is wrong with a world that has left you behind).
**Presence:** The Isolation is the **strongest** Secondary Entity in Roselake. It is the dominant note in the town's ambient fear. Roselake is a dead-end agricultural town with no hospital, one road in or out, and a population that has been slowly declining for decades. The Isolation is woven into the fabric of daily life here.
**Manifestations:** The Isolation has spawned **[[Anglerfish|the Anglerfish]]** — a predatory creature that lures victims in the fields. It has also imbued the **[[Lonely painting (item)|Lonely Painting]]** with supernatural power, turning Elias Peabody's expression of loneliness into a trap that has consumed twelve people.

### The Forgotten
**Associated Fear:** Athazagoraphobia — the fear of being forgotten, ignored, or replaced.
**Composition:** Weighted heavily toward **The Inevitable** (the dread of a slow, irreversible erasure) with undertones of **The Uncanny** (the wrongness of a world where you never existed).
**Presence:** The Forgotten has a growing presence in Roselake, driven by the rising contrast between the world's connectedness and Roselake's disconnectedness, and dramatically intensified by [[Jonathan Oesch]]'s use of [[The Catalyst]].
**Manifestations:** The Catalyst ritual, [[The Case of the Blurred Woman|the Blurred Woman]], and ambient effects that make names harder to remember, photographs seem to fade, and a pervasive sense of insignificance settles over affected areas.
**Full details:** [[The Forgotten]]

### The Unprotected
**Associated Fear:** The fear of being beyond help — that when something goes wrong, no one will come.
**Composition:** Primarily **The Inevitable** (the certainty that disaster is coming) with strong **Predator** undertones (the vulnerability of being exposed and defenseless).
**Presence:** Moderate. Roselake has no hospital. The nearest medical facility is hours away. The fire station covers emergencies as best it can, but everyone in town knows, on some level, that if something really bad happens, help is not coming in time. This fear is a constant, low-level hum.
**Manifestations:** No direct spawns yet, but The Unprotected contributes to the ambient dread that feeds the other Secondary Entities. It makes every other fear worse — isolation is more frightening when you know you're also unprotected.

### The Eye
**Associated Fear:** Scopophobia — the fear of being watched, of surveillance, of the intimate violation of never being alone.
**Composition:** A blend of **The Uncanny** (the wrongness of feeling observed when you should be alone) and **The Inevitable** (the crushing awareness that the watching will never stop).
**Presence:** The Eye does not have a strong presence in Roselake proper — the townspeople are more afraid of being forgotten than of being watched. However, it has a **heavy presence among staff members of the nearby [[Intrusion Institute Monitoring Station|Intrusion Institute]]**, whose operatives live in the world of espionage and surveillance. Their concentrated, professional-grade fear of observation has given The Eye enough of a foothold to spawn a creature.
**Manifestations:** The **[[Mirror Haunt (creature)|Mirror Haunt]]** — a fragile but psychologically devastating entity that inhabits reflective surfaces and feeds on the terror of being watched. Currently targeting [[Mirror Haunt|Edith Zelenskyy]].
