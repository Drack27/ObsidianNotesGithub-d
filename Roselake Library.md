# Roselake Library

**Type:** Public Library
**Location:** Attached to [[Roselake Academy]], town center.
**Former Librarian:** [[Thomas McMullin]]

---

## Description

The Roselake Library is a modest public library attached to Roselake Academy. The town is too small to support a separate library and school, so the two share a building — the library occupies a wing on the east side of the academy, accessible both from the school's interior hallway and from its own entrance on the street.

The library is small by any standard. A single large room with wooden shelving units arranged in rows, a circulation desk near the entrance, a few reading tables with mismatched chairs, and a back room used for storage and the librarian's workspace. The collection is modest but surprisingly well-curated — Thomas McMullin, whatever his eccentricities, took his job seriously and maintained the collection with care.

---

## Collection

The library's holdings include:
- A general fiction and nonfiction collection, skewing toward older titles. New acquisitions are infrequent due to the town's limited budget.
- A reference section including encyclopedias, atlases, and almanacs.
- A small local history section containing county records, old maps, and bound volumes of the Roselake Register dating back decades.
- A handful of books on folklore, the occult, and paranormal phenomena — some of which were Thomas's personal additions to the collection, shelved quietly among the nonfiction.

---

## Historical Records

The library is one of only two locations in town (along with [[Castle Roselake]]) where historical records can be accessed. The local history section contains:
- Bound copies of the **Roselake Register** going back to the early 1900s.
- An **early census record** that includes the names of Roselake's founding-era families.
- **County records** and land transfer documents (partial, overlapping with Castle Roselake's collection).
- A few **church bulletins and community directories** from various decades.

Players investigating any of the historical cases — particularly [[The Case of the Blurred Woman]] — will likely need to visit the library to search these records.

---

## Current State

Since Thomas McMullin's disappearance, a young woman — bookish, slight of frame, with glasses on a chain, apparently in her 20s — has been staffing the circulation desk. She is likely a teacher's aide or school staff member who stepped up informally after Thomas vanished. She knows the regulars by name, directs patrons to the correct sections, and keeps the desk attended during school hours.

She is not a trained librarian in Thomas's mold, and the collection shows no signs of active curation. The back room remains locked. But the library is not simply abandoned — someone is present and managing basic functions.

The library's nature section contains books on predators, pack animals, and large mammals, but nothing in the collection can identify the large, unidentified tooth that [[Agatha Graves]] brought in during [[Session 2]]. Whatever shed that tooth is not in any reference work the library holds.

The library is not supernaturally active. It is simply a small-town library that has lost its caretaker and gained a quiet, informal replacement.

## Investigator Visits

**[[Session 2]] — March 15, 1981:** [[Agatha Graves]] visits the library attempting to identify a large enameled tooth recovered at Mount Roselake (physical evidence of the [[Anglerfish]]). The nature section has no reference material capable of identifying it. The tooth remains unidentified.

**[[Session 7]] — April 4, 1981:** [[Victor Hayes]] and [[Marcus Vale]] visit the library. The librarian on duty directs them to [[Castle Roselake]] for newspaper archives and current-event records. Victor searches the local history section (critical library use success) and finds the following:

- The radio station at [[The Old Radio Tower|Mount Roselake]] was operated by **KGMR**, a rural Nebraska agricultural broadcaster serving the surrounding farming community with weather updates, farm reports, and regional news.
- KGMR operated out of the tower from at least the early 1950s until **1971**, when it relocated to a site closer to Omaha. The move was mundane — better transmitter technology eliminated the need for a remote outpost.
- Radio stations kept **30-day rolling records** of their transmissions as standard practice. The three reel-to-reel tapes recovered from the tower (dated 1950, 1955, and 1961) are almost certainly archived KGMR broadcasts — thirty days of farm reports and weather bulletins, preserved for a decade after the station went dark.

Marcus Vale attempts to access the library's single computer terminal. After waiting out a child who refuses to vacate it, Marcus finds the terminal is a standalone educational machine with no network connection — educational games, Pong, and an ASCII encyclopedia. No useful data.

The library cannot help with current-events research. Both investigators are directed to [[Castle Roselake]] for that purpose.
