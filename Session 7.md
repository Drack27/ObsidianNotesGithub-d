# Session 7

**Date (in-game):** April 4, 1981
**Player Characters:** [[Victor Hayes]], [[Marcus Vale]]
**Players:** Kelvin (Victor), Alex (Marcus)

---

## Context: The 17-Day Gap

The session opened with a montage covering March 18 through April 3, 1981 — seventeen days of in-game time elapsed to bring Victor and Marcus into sync with the end of [[Session 6]].

[[Victor Hayes]] spent this period doing what he'd been building toward: working through the full stack of case files he'd taken from the radio tower, visiting the library at [[Roselake Academy]], and generally trying to orient himself within a small town that was quietly coming apart. During this time he also tried to do something with the mystery machine and the VHS tapes he'd scavenged from the dumpster behind Charlie's Quick Stop.

The mystery machine turned out to be a broken VHS player. Victor found his way to **[[Sam]]** at [[The Convenience and Video Rental Store|Charlie's Quick Stop]], who sold him the parts to get it running and helped put it together. With a working player in hand, Victor was able to screen the two gunky VHS tapes he'd found in the dumpster:

- One contained the 1979 film ***All That Jazz***
- One contained a martial arts film, labeled *Enter the Dragon*

Neither tape contained anything useful to the investigation.

The three reel-to-reel tapes recovered from the radio tower (dated 1961, 1955, and 1950) remain unplayed — the Quick Stop's VHS equipment cannot accommodate them.

Toward the end of March, **[[Marcus Vale]]** arrived in Roselake on a work contract. Victor and Marcus already knew each other through government work, and Victor had called in the connection: he needed someone who could make sense of the electronic equipment and recordings he'd been accumulating. Marcus, a computer programmer with military contractor experience, settled into a room with a local farm couple — **Francine and Tom** — and got to work. By April 4, he'd gone through the tapes and the mystery machine and confirmed there wasn't much there.

---

## Events

### Morning: Charlie's Quick Stop

Victor and Marcus drove to [[The Convenience and Video Rental Store|Charlie's Quick Stop]] in Victor's large SUV. Victor filled the tank; Marcus went inside to ask about equipment.

The teenager working the gas station counter — long dark hair, reading a fashion magazine, systematically ignoring all customers — barely registered their presence. Victor recognized her from one of Thomas McMullin's case file photographs: this was **Sylvia Lemon**, documented in the files as a local girl who had disappeared for two days and returned behaving differently. She is still working the register. She is not the girl described in the church directories and school records — not warm, not active, not engaged. Whatever came back from those two days is not what left.

They moved into **Sam's Video Rentals**, the corrugated metal annex. Low lighting, psychedelic patterning on the carpet, movie posters covering every wall — *The Thing*, *Alien*, *Jaws*, a genuine enthusiast's shrine. **[[Sam Reardon]]** was in the back room marked "Repairs." He emerged with his usual frenetic energy and was happy to see them.

Victor handed Sam one of the reel-to-reel tapes — the one dated **December 29, 1950** — to examine. Sam was surprised; they're professional broadcasting equipment, genuinely strange to find sitting in a private collection. He asked where Victor had gotten them. Victor attempted to explain (fast talk, twice, both failures) and came up short both times. Sam noticed but let it go. His conclusion: he doesn't have the playback equipment for reel-to-reel, and there's nothing in Roselake that does. He suggested that if Victor found a reel-to-reel player — maybe in an attic somewhere — he could help set it up.

They left without answers for the tapes.

### Midday: Tony's Diner

Victor and Marcus drove to [[Tony's Diner]] for lunch.

Victor had been coming through here regularly over his weeks in town, and he noticed the shift before the food arrived: the mood was lower than it had been. Tony was still working the counter — big laugh, warm handshake for the farmers — but when no one was looking directly at him, the performance dropped. He looked haggard. He looked tired in a way that goes beyond one bad night.

Their waitress was **Tanya**, who served them without issue and took their orders: a grilled cheese with tomato soup (Marcus) and a burger with no pickles plus a Shirley Temple (Victor). The food was good. Victor left a heavy tip.

When the lunch rush thinned, they bribed Tanya — a little cash, a softer question — to talk. What she shared:

- **Murders and disappearances.** This wasn't news, but it was the first time someone in town had said it plainly. People are going missing. There have been killings.
- **The truck driver.** A delivery driver — not a local, just someone who ran a regular route — was found dead in the street. The Sheriff looked into it and then didn't. Nobody followed up.
- **The Sheriff is struggling.** He comes into the diner occasionally. He doesn't look well. He's the only lawman here, with no real support, and the town is sliding out from under him.
- **The silence is spreading.** People used to talk in here. They're talking less. They sit with their food and look at their plates.

Tanya found the conversation uncomfortable and ended it. She had no interest in being the source on any of this and made clear she wasn't going to say more. Victor did a visual sweep of the room on the way out — nothing overtly wrong, just the ambient tension that had settled over the whole place.

### Afternoon: Roselake Academy / Public Library

Victor and Marcus drove to [[Roselake Academy]], parked, and walked to the [[Roselake Library|Public Library wing]].

The librarian on duty — a young woman, late 20s or early 30s, chain glasses, quiet — welcomed them and pointed them toward the stacks when they described what they were after. For newspaper records and town documents dated to current events, she directed them to **[[Castle Roselake]]** (the town hall, which holds the Roselake Register archives, the Mayor's office, and the town records).

The library itself, however, had something Victor wanted.

#### Victor's Library Research (Critical Success)

Digging through the local history and periodical holdings, Victor found what he needed to place the reel-to-reel tapes in context:

**The radio station at [[The Old Radio Tower|Mount Roselake]] was operated by KGMR** — a rural Nebraska agricultural radio station that broadcast weather updates, farm reports, and general regional news to the surrounding area. KGMR ran out of the tower from at least the early 1950s until **1971**, when the station relocated closer to Omaha. The move was mundane: better transmitter technology meant they no longer needed to maintain an outpost in the boonies to reach their coverage area.

More usefully: **radio stations kept 30-day rolling records of their transmissions** as standard practice. The reel-to-reel tapes — dated 1950, 1955, and 1961 — are almost certainly archived broadcast recordings from KGMR's operational years. Thirty days of daily farm reports and weather bulletins, preserved in that rusted box in the basement for a decade after the station went dark.

Whether those broadcasts contain anything relevant is unknown. The tapes can't be played without a reel-to-reel machine.

#### Marcus's Computer Attempt

The library's one computer — a boxy terminal running educational software — was occupied by a young boy (Carl Weezer type, glasses, very protective of his Pong game) when Marcus arrived at it. Marcus failed to dislodge him through intimidation. He waited fifteen minutes. The bell rang; the boy was physically pried off the keyboard by the librarian and shooed to class.

Marcus got on the computer. He found: typing games, Pong, and a primitive ASCII encyclopedia with pictures of giraffes. No network connection. No administrative files. No link to any external system. He attempted to access whatever deeper systems might exist — there were none. This was a standalone educational terminal, full stop.

They agreed to call it a day. Next stop: **[[Castle Roselake]]**, to go through the town records and newspaper archives.

## Significance

Session 7 is the first session involving both Victor Hayes and a new player character — [[Marcus Vale]] — and establishes their working relationship. Marcus is a late introduction to the campaign, brought in by Victor specifically because the investigation had run into a wall of hardware (the reel-to-reel tapes, the mystery machine) that Victor couldn't crack alone.

The session's key finding is the identity of the radio station: **KGMR**, confirmed by library records, explains what the reel-to-reel tapes are (30-day broadcast archives) and when they were made (operational years ending 1971). They still can't be played, but the context is now established.

Victor recognized **Sylvia Lemon** at the gas station counter — a face from Thomas McMullin's files, a name that matches the pattern of disappearance-and-return documented in the Skinwalkers folder. Whatever she is now, she's still in place, still working the register.

**[[Castle Roselake]]** (town hall) is the next destination: newspaper archives, town records, and current-event documentation are all held there. Whatever the library couldn't tell them about March and April of 1981, the town archives might.

Meanwhile, **Matthew Davis** — last seen unconscious on the floor of his living room after [[Jonathan Oesch]]'s attack on the night of April 3 — is not mentioned. Victor and Marcus don't know he exists, and his fate after Session 6 remains unresolved.
