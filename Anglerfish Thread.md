# PART ONE — Thomas's File

_What Thomas McMullin has on his wall: photographs, index cards, notes, timelines. This is what players/readers would encounter if they looked through his materials._

## Thomas's Theory

Thomas believes people around town have been disappearing briefly, then reappearing _changed_ — replaced by something he's calling "skinwalkers." He thinks they're building numbers, and that eventually they'll rise up and take over the town once there are enough of them. He has photographs and descriptions of suspected individuals pinned all over his wall, tracking them before and after their behavioral shifts.

He doesn't know _what_ is doing the replacing, or _how._ He just knows people are being swapped.

## The Suspects

Thomas treats all of the following as confirmed or likely skinwalkers. He does not distinguish between real replacements and false leads — to him, they're all part of the same pattern.

**Sylvia Lemmon** — 17, east side of town. Disappeared for 2 days about 25 days ago, then reappeared acting noticeably different — more rebellious, more defiant. Her parents are making a stink about it. Thomas has her flagged as one of the earliest swaps he noticed.

**Wendy Simons** — 32, cashier at Joe's Superstore. Didn't show up to work for a few days. When she came back, coworkers noticed she was acting normal but **monotone** — performing her job mechanically, like a robot. It creeps people out. She still goes home and feeds her cat, but something is clearly off.

**Zacharias ("Zach") & Toula Carmody** — Married couple, both changed around the same time. Their children are grown and out of state, so nobody noticed them go missing. But someone at church noticed they've stopped speaking entirely — they used to love talking and socializing. Now they're completely closed off. Thomas has been watching them closely.

**Donny McPhee** — Farmer. Became suddenly and noticeably grouchy. People are talking about the personality shift. Thomas has him on the wall. _(Note: entry incomplete — flesh out what Thomas has observed and written down about him.)_

**Winfred (a dog)** — Thomas is also tracking a dog whose owners reported a sudden behavioral change. The dog now moves stiffly and acts pitifully. Thomas considers it possible that the skinwalkers aren't limited to humans.

**Mayor Lance Winnipeg** — 37. Thomas's most recent and most alarming addition to the file. The mayor has been acting impulsive and loud — no volume control, acting without thinking — when he was previously known as kindly and community-minded. People are _definitely_ noticing. Thomas is extremely alarmed by this one.

# PART TWO — What's Actually Happening

_The full truth behind each case. Thomas does not have access to any of this information._

## The Anglerfish

The anglerfish has been killing people around town and replacing them with **skin puppets** — hollow replicas that take the victim's place.

## Skin Puppet Properties

- They are passable mimics — okay at imitating the person they replaced, but not great.
- They talk as little as possible and try to maintain general human appearances.
- They **cannot learn anything new.** They run only on whatever the original person already knew.
- If cut open, they are filled with a **black and gray ashy, powdery substance** (sawdust-like texture).
- If struck, they feel **very solid.**
- People close to the victim can tell _something_ has changed, but can't pinpoint exactly what.

## The Truth About Each Suspect

### Actually Replaced (Skin Puppets)

**Wendy Simons** — Genuinely bitten and replaced. Her monotone, robotic behavior is the skin puppet running on Wendy's existing knowledge and routines, unable to adapt or grow. The mimicry is functional but hollow.

**Zach & Toula Carmody** — Both bitten simultaneously. Their refusal to speak at church is the skin puppets' limitation — they default to silence to avoid exposure. The couple's social nature made the contrast especially obvious.

### Not Replaced (False Leads)

**Sylvia Lemmon** — Not a skinwalker. She went on a wild road trip with friends (drinking, partying) and came back more rebellious. Her parents' reaction is what put her on Thomas's radar. She's just a teenager.

**Donny McPhee** — Not a skinwalker. He broke his leg, splinted it himself, and is hiding the injury. The pain is making him grouchy. _(Note: entry incomplete — needs more red-herring detail.)_

**Winfred (the dog)** — Not a skinwalker. Suffering from a rare joint disease with sudden onset. The stiff movement and pitiful behavior are symptoms, not a replacement. Just a sick dog.

### Not Yet Bitten — Future Target

**Mayor Lance Winnipeg** — Has _not_ been bitten yet. He is currently still himself. However, if nothing intervenes, he is likely to be lured in and bitten within the next few weeks. Thomas's alarm about the mayor is, ironically, premature — but prophetic.

His eventual skin shell (if it happens) would be impulsive, loud, and erratic — a dramatic contrast to his current kindly, community-minded personality. People would notice immediately.
