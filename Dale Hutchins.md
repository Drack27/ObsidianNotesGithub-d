# Dale Hutchins

**Age:** Early 40s
**Residence:** A house on the north side of Roselake, with his wife and two children (ages 6 and 12).
**Occupation:** Works at the grain elevator.
**Case Connection:** [[The Scratching]]

---

## Description

Dale Hutchins is a quiet, practical man — the kind of person who keeps to himself, does his work at the grain elevator, and doesn't cause trouble. He's not a drinker, not excitable, and not the type to make things up or exaggerate. If Dale Hutchins tells you something is wrong, something is wrong.

Which is why the fact that he's been telling people at the diner that ghosts are infesting his house has caught people's attention.

## Family

Dale lives with his wife and their two children — a six-year-old and a twelve-year-old. The children are the primary drivers of the supernatural misinterpretation of events in the Hutchins household. The twelve-year-old is old enough to build convincing narratives about what they've heard and seen, and the six-year-old is young enough to believe every word of it. Between the two of them, they have spun the raccoon infestation into a full-blown ghost story that has consumed the family's imagination.

The parents were initially skeptical. Dale and his wife scolded the children for making things up and even grounded them at one point for what they assumed were pranks (scattered food, knocked-over trash cans). That all changed the night of the **floating brick incident** — after that, the parents' skepticism gave way to genuine fear, and the family united around the conviction that something paranormal was happening in their home.

## The Floating Brick Incident

Dale put a brick on the kitchen trash can lid to stop whatever was getting into the garbage. One night, his wife came downstairs for a midnight snack. She turned the corner into the kitchen just in time to see the brick **"float" off the garbage can** and land on the floor.

She screamed. It became a whole-family incident — kids woken up, lights on, everyone in the kitchen, the six-year-old crying, the twelve-year-old saying "I told you so."

The truth: the mother raccoon, hidden in the shadows of the unlit kitchen, pushed the brick off the lid. The garbage can was black and invisible in the dark. The brick, lighter in color, caught the moonlight coming through the kitchen window. For a split second, from the doorway, it looked exactly like the brick levitated off an invisible surface and dropped to the floor.

This moment is the turning point of the case. Before this, the parents thought the children were being dramatic. After this, the entire family believed.

## Current State

Dale is sleep-deprived, frustrated, and increasingly convinced that the "whispering" he hears through the walls at night is supernatural in origin. The raccoons' chittering and trilling, filtered through insulation and drywall, sounds convincingly like muffled whispering in the dead of night — and the family's chronic lack of sleep is making their judgment worse.

He has been working with [[Thomas McMullin]] for several weeks to a month, during which time Thomas has never managed to catch or even see any of the raccoons, which has only reinforced the belief that they are dealing with something incorporeal.

Dale's primary descriptor for the sounds is: **"that G** D*** whispering."**
