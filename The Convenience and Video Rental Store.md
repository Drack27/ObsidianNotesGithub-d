# Charlie's Quick Stop & Sam's Video Rentals

**Type:** Gas Station, Retail & Entertainment
**Location:** [[Town Center]], Roselake
**Owner:** Charlie (Video Rentals)

---

## Description

A strange hybrid business. The main building is **Charlie's Quick Stop**, a brick 7-Eleven-style gas station and convenience store with two gas pumps, an ice machine, and a payphone out front. It sells typical gas station inventory.

Attached to the side of the Quick Stop is a corrugated metal shack, haphazardly stapled on, serving as **Sam's Video Rentals**. Despite the crude exterior, the inside features a dimly lit, psychedelic colorful patterned floor and a surprisingly well-stocked VHS rental library.

---

## Charlie Haverford

The store is owned and operated by **Charlie Haverford**, a 60-year-old retired electrical engineer who moved to Roselake in the mid-1970s after a career working for a telecommunications company in Omaha. Charlie is an eccentric, enthusiastic early adopter of home video technology who became convinced that VHS was the future of entertainment and sank his entire retirement savings into building what he considers the finest video rental collection in rural Nebraska.

He is not wrong about the quality of his collection — it is genuinely impressive, spanning hundreds of titles across every genre, meticulously organized and maintained. He is, however, somewhat wrong about the business prospects of a VHS rental store in a town of 651 people. The store survives largely because the convenience store half generates enough revenue to keep the lights on, and because Charlie doesn't need the money — he does this for love.

Charlie is friendly, talkative, and eager to recommend films to anyone who walks in. He is one of the few people in Roselake who seems genuinely happy with his life, which makes him either the sanest person in town or the most oblivious.

## Investigator Visits

**[[Session 4]] — March 17, 1981 (late night):** [[Victor Hayes]] visits the Quick Stop. He interacts with the bored teenager working the night shift. While scoping out the back alley, Victor spots the [[Anglerfish]] (a bear-sized, vantablack entity) slinking away in posession of Thomas McMullin's skin as it's lure. After waiting for it to leave, Victor successfully dumpster dives behind the building, recovering two gunky VHS tapes, a screwdriver, and a mysterious crud-covered electronic machine.

**March 18 – April 3, 1981 (between sessions):** Victor contacts Charlie and purchases parts to repair the recovered VHS machine, which Charlie helps him restore. The two gunky dumpster tapes are screened and found to contain commercial films with no investigative value. Three reel-to-reel tapes recovered from the radio tower remain unplayable — Charlie has no equipment for them.

**[[Session 7]] — April 4, 1981 (morning):** Victor and [[Marcus Vale]] visit the Quick Stop together. Victor recognizes the teenage girl working the counter as **Sylvia Lemon**, whose photograph appeared in Thomas McMullin's case files — a local documented as having disappeared for two days and returned changed. In the back repair room, Victor shows Charlie one of the reel-to-reel tapes (dated December 29, 1950). Charlie can't play it and has no equipment that can — he suggests finding a reel-to-reel player locally (perhaps in someone's attic) and brings it to him. Victor's attempts to explain where the tape came from fail; Charlie notices but lets it go. They leave without progress on the tapes.
