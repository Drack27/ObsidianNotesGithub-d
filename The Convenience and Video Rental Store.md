# Charlie's Quick Stop & Sam's Video Rentals

**Type:** Gas Station, Retail & Entertainment
**Location:** [[Town Center]], Roselake
**Owners:** Charlie Haverford (Quick Stop) / Sam Reardon (Video Rentals)

---

## Description

A strange hybrid business. The main building is **Charlie's Quick Stop**, a brick 7-Eleven-style gas station and convenience store with two gas pumps, an ice machine, and a payphone out front. It sells typical gas station inventory.

Attached to the side of the Quick Stop is a corrugated metal shack, haphazardly stapled on, serving as **Sam's Video Rentals**. Despite the crude exterior, the inside features a dimly lit, psychedelic colorful patterned floor and a surprisingly well-stocked VHS rental library.

---

## Charlie Haverford

The Quick Stop is owned and operated by **Charlie Haverford**, a middle-aged man who has been running the gas station long enough that it has become an extension of himself — functional, slightly run-down, and not particularly interested in impressing anyone. Charlie is practical and keeps to himself. The video rental shack attached to his building is Sam Reardon's operation; Charlie's role there is landlord, not enthusiast.

He is friendly enough if you need something, but he is not the person to ask about films.

---

## Sam Reardon

**[[Sam Reardon]]** runs Sam's Video Rentals out of the corrugated metal annex. He is young, blonde, and operates at a frequency most people in Roselake don't quite share — fast-talking, frenetic, constitutionally incapable of a short answer when the topic is film or electronics. His shop reflects his personality: dimly lit, psychedelic floor, movie posters covering every wall (*The Thing*, *Alien*, *Jaws*). A genuine enthusiast's shrine.

Sam is also the person to see for consumer electronics repair. The back room, marked "Repairs," is his natural habitat.

---

## Relevance

The store is a minor social gathering point and a potential source of casual information. Sam, unlike Charlie, talks — though his information tends to be filtered through his enthusiasm for movies and hardware. ("You know, what you're describing sounds a lot like the plot of *Invasion of the Body Snatchers*...")

## Investigator Visits

**[[Session 4]] — March 17, 1981 (late night):** [[Victor Hayes]] visits the Quick Stop. He interacts with the bored teenager running the gas station and observes the corrugated metal shack attached to the side serving as Sam's Video Rentals. While investigating the alleys around the store, he spots the [[Anglerfish]] (a bear-sized, vantablack entity) slinking away with the deflated skin of Thomas McMullin as its lure. After waiting for it to leave, Victor successfully dumpster dives behind the building, recovering two gunky VHS tapes, a screwdriver, and a mysterious crud-covered electronic machine.

**March 18 – April 3, 1981 (between sessions):** Victor & Marcus Vale bring the mystery machine to Sam, who identifies it as a broken VHS player, sources parts, and helps them repair it. The two gunky dumpster tapes are screened and contain only commercial films. Three reel-to-reel tapes recovered from the radio tower remain unplayable — Sam has no equipment for them.

**[[Session 7]] — April 4, 1981 (morning):** Victor and [[Marcus Vale]] visit the Quick Stop together. Victor recognizes the teenage girl working the gas station counter as **Sylvia Lemon**, whose photograph appeared in Thomas McMullin's case files — a local documented as having disappeared for two days and returned changed. They move into Sam's Video Rentals. Sam is in the back repair room; he emerges with his usual frenetic energy and is happy to see them. Victor hands Sam one of the reel-to-reel tapes (dated December 29, 1950). Sam is surprised; it's professional broadcast equipment. He asks where Victor got it. Victor's attempts to explain fail twice; Sam notices but lets it go. His conclusion: no reel-to-reel playback equipment exists in Roselake. He suggests finding a player somewhere — maybe an attic — and bringing it to him. They leave without progress on the tapes.
