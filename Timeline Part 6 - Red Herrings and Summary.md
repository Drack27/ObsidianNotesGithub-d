### THE WEEPING HOUSE — Marsh Residence (Red Herring, Active ~January–March 1981)

- **Location:** Marsh house, south end of Roselake. Built early 1900s.
- **Residents:** Reuben & Clara Marsh (late 50s). Together over 20 years in the same house.
- **Spiritual Background:** Joined new age movement in early 1960s (tarot, crystals, seance room, chakra books). Recently and abruptly shifted to Catholic paraphernalia — crosses on nearly every wall, demonology books, rosary on rearview mirror, holy water by the front door.

#### Reported Phenomena (last ~2 months):
| Phenomenon | Supernatural Interpretation | Actual Cause |
|---|---|---|
| Banging/knocking in walls | Demonic pounding | Water hammer from corroding galvanized pipes |
| Blood-red water from taps | "Blood" — demonic omen | Rust/iron oxide from galvanized pipe corrosion |
| Cold spots (upstairs hallway, back bedroom) | Ghostly presence | Pinhole leaks in corroded pipes allowing outside air into wall cavities |
| Sulfurous smell | The Devil's presence (brimstone) | Hydrogen sulfide from sulfate-reducing bacteria in corroding pipes |
| Spreading ceiling stain (upstairs hallway) | Ectoplasm / demonic manifestation | Slow leak from a corroded pipe joint |

#### Character Notes:
- **Reuben:** More practical by nature; was dragged into spiritualism by Clara over the years. Now terrified and angry that no one takes their situation seriously.
- **Clara:** Always the spiritual one. Describes the water as "blood." Buys crosses and demonology books. Fully convinced the house is demonically infested.
- Both have latched onto the demonic interpretation because it gives their fear a shape — at least demons are something the Church has fought before.

#### Root Cause:
- Original galvanized steel plumbing from the early 1900s has corroded over 80+ years.
- **Fix:** Full replumb of the house. Expensive but straightforward. Zero supernatural involvement.

#### Teaching Purpose:
- Not everything is supernatural. Some things are just old and broken.

### THE SCRATCHING — Hutchins Residence (Red Herring, Active ~February–March 1981)

- **Location:** Hutchins family house, north side of Roselake.
- **Residents:** Dale Hutchins (early 40s, grain elevator worker), wife, two children (ages 6 and 12).
- **Investigator:** Thomas McMullin has spent several weeks trying to catch the source. Completely unsuccessful. Becoming convinced it's supernatural.

#### Reported Phenomena:
| Phenomenon | Supernatural Interpretation | Actual Cause |
|---|---|---|
| Scratching in walls/ceiling at night | Entity trapped in walls | Raccoon movement through attic/wall spaces |
| "Whispering" sounds | Ghostly voices | Raccoon vocalizations filtered through insulation |
| Food disturbances in pantry | Poltergeist activity | Mother raccoon raiding the pantry |
| Floating brick incident | Telekinesis / poltergeist | Mother raccoon pushing a brick off a trash can in the dark kitchen; moonlight caught the brick mid-fall, creating the illusion of levitation |
| Foul smell | Ectoplasm / death | Accumulated raccoon urine and droppings in attic |
| Claw marks on attic hatch | Entity trying to break through | Raccoon claws pushing from above |

#### Root Cause:
- A mother raccoon and her kits entered the house through a gap in the soffit.
- They have nested in the attic and are foraging through the house at night.

#### Character Notes:
- **Dale Hutchins:** Quiet, practical, not the type to make things up. When he says something's wrong, people believe him. Currently sleep-deprived, frustrated, and genuinely frightened. His primary complaint: *"That God damn whispering."*
- **Thomas McMullin:** Has developed an overdeveloped, thick case file on this. Weeks of effort without catching a single raccoon has pushed him toward supernatural explanations.

#### Teaching Purpose:
- Not everything is supernatural. Also demonstrates that Thomas McMullin — the primary investigator across all cases — can be wrong.
