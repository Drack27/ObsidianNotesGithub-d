# The Catalyst

**Type:** Ritual Spell
**Origin:** Insane inspiration channeled from [[The Forgotten]]
**Known Practitioner:** [[Jonathan Oesch]]
**Case Connection:** [[The Antimemetic Killer]], [[The Case of the Blurred Woman]]

---

## Overview

The Catalyst is a ritual spell that erases a target from the memories of everyone who ever knew them. It does not kill. It does not teleport. It simply makes the world forget that a person exists — painting them out of every memory, every recollection, every mental image, as thoroughly as if they had been airbrushed out of a photograph.

The knowledge of how to perform this spell was granted to Jonathan Oesch through insane inspiration channeled from **The Forgotten**, one of the Secondary Entities active in Roselake. Jonathan did not learn it from a book or a teacher — it arrived in his mind fully formed, a gift from something that wanted to be used.

---

## Requirements

The Catalyst requires the following components:

### 1. A Brain
The brain of an animal **at least as intelligent as a pig.** The strength of the brain determines how deeply the spell can sever social bonds:
- **Pig brain** — Sufficient to erase casual acquaintances, coworkers, and community-level connections. People who knew the target in passing will forget them entirely.
- **Human brain** — Far more powerful. Can sever family ties, deep friendships, and intimate bonds. Even people who loved the target deeply will lose all memory of them.

The brain must be fresh and intact. It is consumed by the ritual.

### 2. A Likeness
A representational image of the target — a **photograph, drawing, carving, or any likeness** that depicts the target's face or form. It does not need to be a perfect likeness, but it must be recognizably the target.

### 3. The Circle and Epithets
A circle must be drawn around the likeness, inscribed with **epithets calling upon The Forgotten.** These are titles and invocations of the entity:
- *"It That Erases All"*
- *"The Annihilator"*
- *"Think No More Of"*
- *"It That Erases All That Is Love"*
- *"Great Non-Socializer"*

Jonathan writes these in Latin — not because Latin is a requirement of the spell, but because it feels right to him. Any language would work.

### 4. Abandonment
The completed ritual — brain, likeness, circle, and epithets — must be **left somewhere that even the caster forgets about it.** This is the cruelest requirement: the spell demands that its own existence be forgotten. It feeds on forgetting, and it begins with the caster.

Jonathan handles this by setting reminders for himself on a clock by his bedside table, which alerts him approximately once a week to check on his ritual site at [[The Collapsed Farmhouse]].

---

## Effects

Once the Catalyst takes effect:

- **All associates, friends, and family** of the target lose their memories of the target. The target is not simply suppressed — they are *painted out*, as if airbrushed from the recollections of everyone who ever interacted with them.
- **Existing memories are altered**, not simply deleted. A family dinner becomes a dinner with an empty chair that no one questions. A group photo becomes a photo with a gap that no one notices.
- **Physical records are not affected.** Newspaper articles, photographs, church directories, public records — these remain intact. The Catalyst erases memory, not paper. This is how Thomas McMullin was able to discover the disappearances.
- The strength of the brain determines the **depth of erasure.** A pig brain leaves strong personal bonds partially intact (though the person may feel confused or uneasy about memories they can't quite place). A human brain severs everything.

## Irreversibility

The Catalyst cannot be reversed, dispelled, or undone. There is no counter-ritual, no cleansing, no supernatural mechanism that restores what was erased. The forgetting is permanent.

The only way to restore someone's awareness of a forgotten person is the mundane way — by reintroducing them to evidence of that person's existence. Newspaper clippings, photographs, public records, personal testimony. You teach them about the forgotten person the same way you would teach anyone about a person they've never heard of: you show them proof, you tell them stories, you make the case that this person was real. It is slow, painstaking work, and it will never fully replicate the original memories — those are gone. But it can rebuild awareness, recognition, and connection from scratch.

This is, in a sense, the cruelest aspect of the spell. It doesn't destroy the forgotten person. It doesn't even destroy the evidence. It just severs the human threads that connected them to the world — and leaves the work of reconnection entirely to whoever cares enough to try.

## Call of Cthulhu 7th Edition Mechanics

**Type:** Ritual Spell
**Cost:** 12 magic points, 1D6 Sanity
**Casting Time:** Approximately 1 hour of ritual preparation and inscription
**Components:** Animal brain (minimum pig intelligence), likeness of target, inscribed circle with epithets

The caster must succeed on a **POW roll** for the ritual to take effect. If the roll fails, the components are consumed but nothing happens — the brain is wasted, and the caster must acquire a new one.

On a success, the spell takes effect over the following 24–48 hours as memories of the target dissolve from the minds of everyone who knew them. The caster loses the specified magic points and Sanity at the time of casting.

**Brain Strength and Depth of Erasure:**
- **Pig brain (or equivalent):** Erases casual acquaintances, coworkers, and community-level connections. Deep personal bonds (close family, intimate friends) may resist, leaving the bonded person with a persistent, uncomfortable sense that something is missing — though they cannot identify what.
- **Human brain:** Erases all connections, including family ties, deep friendships, and intimate bonds. No relationship is strong enough to resist.

**Sanity Note for Victims:** A person who has been targeted by the Catalyst does not lose Sanity from the spell itself — they are unaware anything has happened. However, the slow realization that no one recognizes them, that their name provokes blank stares, that they have been unmade from the world's memory, may warrant Sanity rolls at the Keeper's discretion as the horror of their situation becomes clear.

## Connection to The Forgotten

The Catalyst is not merely a spell — it is a **channel for The Forgotten's influence.** Every use of the Catalyst strengthens The Forgotten's presence in Roselake. The fear of being forgotten that the spell generates — in the victims, in the peripheral unease it creates in the community — feeds the entity.

Jonathan Oesch is, in a sense, an unwitting agent of The Forgotten. He believes he is wielding a tool for his own purposes — revenge against a community that rejected him. In reality, he is doing exactly what The Forgotten wants: making forgetting *real*, making it *happen*, and generating fear in the process.

---

## Connection to The Blurred Woman

The Catalyst and [[The Case of the Blurred Woman|The Blurred Woman]] are connected through The Forgotten. The Blurred Woman is a pre-existing pseudo-avatar of The Forgotten — a woman from the 1800s who was deliberately forgotten by her community and now exists in a limbo state between the physical and supernatural realms. The Catalyst's use has intensified The Forgotten's presence in Roselake, creating more "weak spots" where the Blurred Woman can manifest.
