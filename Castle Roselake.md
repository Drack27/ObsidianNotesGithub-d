# Castle Roselake

**Type:** Municipal Building
**Location:** Roselake town center.
**Case Connections:** [[The Case of the Blurred Woman]], [[The Old Cemetary]]

---

## Description

"Castle Roselake" is the somewhat mocking, cute local nickname for the town's municipal building — a squat, unremarkable brick structure that houses both the **Post Office** and the **Mayor's Office.** The nickname comes from the building's pretensions: someone, decades ago, added a crenellated parapet to the roofline, presumably to give the town's seat of government a sense of grandeur. The effect is more endearing than impressive. It looks like a post office wearing a Halloween costume.

The building is one of the older structures in Roselake, dating to the early twentieth century. Inside, it's all scuffed linoleum, buzzing fluorescent lights, and filing cabinets that haven't been reorganized since the Eisenhower administration.

---

## Layout

- **Ground floor:** The Post Office occupies the front half. The back half is a small office for the town clerk and a records storage room.
- **Second floor:** The Mayor's Office and a small meeting room that doubles as the town council chamber on the rare occasions the council convenes.
- **Basement:** File storage. Decades of municipal records, county correspondence, land deeds, and bureaucratic minutiae in varying states of organization.

---

## Records

Castle Roselake's basement contains the oldest surviving municipal records for the town, dating back to the late 1800s. These records are disorganized, incomplete, and in some cases water-damaged, but they include:

- **Land deeds and property transfers** — who owned what, and when.
- **County correspondence** — letters between Roselake's officials and the county seat.
- **Census and population records** — partial, but useful for identifying residents from specific eras.
- **Burial records** — referencing plots at [[The Old Cemetary]], though these records are fragmentary for the oldest burials (1840s-1880s).
- **Miscellaneous files** — complaints, permits, tax records, and the accumulated paper trail of a small town's existence.

## Relevance to Cases

### The Blurred Woman
Players investigating [[The Case of the Blurred Woman]] may need to search the municipal records for information about a woman from the mid-to-late 1800s. The burial records, census data, and land deeds stored in Castle Roselake's basement could help identify who the Blurred Woman was in life — her name, her dates, her relations, and the circumstances of her death.

The challenge is that the records from this era are sparse and poorly organized. A dedicated search could take hours, and there's no guarantee the relevant documents survived.

### General Research
Castle Roselake is one of only two locations in town (along with the [[Roselake Library]]) where historical records can be accessed. Players conducting research into Roselake's history — for any case — will likely end up here at some point.

## Atmosphere

Castle Roselake is not supernaturally active. It is, however, profoundly bureaucratic. The building has the energy of a place that processes forms and stores paperwork and has done so for over a century. The basement, in particular, has a musty, forgotten quality — rows of filing cabinets and cardboard boxes full of records that no one has looked at in decades. The feeling of being surrounded by forgotten names and forgotten business is atmospheric, if not supernatural.
