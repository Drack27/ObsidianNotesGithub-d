## Thomas's Notes

_A single page, handwritten in blue ink. The writing is less structured than his other case files — more like field notes jotted down quickly._

---

> **THE SCRATCHING — DALE HUTCHINS' HOUSE** _North side of town_
> 
> Dale Hutchins, early 40s, lives alone. Quiet guy — works at the grain elevator, keeps to himself, not a drinker, not excitable. If Dale says something is wrong, something is wrong.
> 
> Dale says something is living in his walls.
> 
> What he's told me:
> 
> — Scratching sounds. Walls and ceiling. Mostly at night. It MOVES — starts on one side of the house, travels to the other. Sometimes frantic bursts. Sometimes slow and deliberate.
> 
> — Chittering. High-pitched, chattering sounds. Dale says it's "almost like whispering." Loudest when the house is dead quiet. Late at night.
> 
> — Something is getting into his FOOD. He's woken up to find the pantry door open, bread torn apart, dry goods scattered on the kitchen floor. Happened at least 4 times. He put a brick on the kitchen trash lid. It was knocked over anyway.
> 
> — Smell. Sharp, musky, ammonia. Strongest near the attic hatch in the upstairs hall. Wasn't there a month ago. Getting worse.
> 
> — Claw marks on the attic hatch. From above. (See photo.)
> 
> I went over last Tuesday. Confirmed the smell — it's BAD. Saw the scratches on the hatch. While I was standing in the upstairs hallway, I heard rustling from above. Faint but definite. Something up there shifted its weight.
> 
> I did NOT open the attic. Dale hasn't either. He says he started to once and the scratching got loud and fast and he backed off.
> 
> Dale's theory: rats. Big ones. My theory: I don't know. Rats don't open pantry doors. Rats don't knock over a trash can weighted with a brick. And rats don't make sounds that a grown man describes as "whispering."
> 
> Whatever this is, it's smart. It's strong. And it's comfortable.
