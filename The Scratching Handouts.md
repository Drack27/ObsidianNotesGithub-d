## Thomas's Notes

_Multiple pages of handwritten notes, spanning several weeks of investigation. The writing ranges from structured case notes to increasingly frustrated field observations._

### Page 1 — "THE SCRATCHING — HUTCHINS FAMILY"

> **THE HUTCHINS FAMILY** — North side of town.
>
> [[Dale Hutchins]], early 40s, works at the grain elevator. His wife. Two kids, ages 6 and 12. Dale is not the type to make things up. If Dale says something is wrong, something is wrong.
>
> The family says something is living in their walls.
>
> What they've told me:
>
> — Scratching sounds. Walls and ceiling. Mostly at night. It MOVES — starts on one side of the house, travels to the other. Sometimes frantic bursts. Sometimes slow and deliberate.
>
> — "That G** D*** whispering." Dale's words. Late at night, when the house is dead quiet, something in the walls makes sounds that, through the insulation and drywall, sound exactly like someone whispering. It keeps the whole family awake. The kids are terrified. Dale and his wife are exhausted.
>
> — Something is getting into their FOOD. Pantry door open, bread torn apart, dry goods scattered on the kitchen floor. Happened at least 4 times. Dale put a brick on the kitchen trash lid. It was knocked over anyway.
>
> — THE BRICK INCIDENT: Dale's wife came down for a midnight snack. Kitchen was dark. She turned the corner and saw the brick FLOAT off the garbage can and drop to the floor. She screamed. Whole family woke up. Before this, the parents thought the kids were pulling pranks and even grounded them. After this, EVERYONE is convinced the house is haunted.
>
> — Smell. Sharp, acrid, biological. Strongest near the attic hatch in the upstairs hall. Getting worse by the week.
>
> — Claw marks on the attic hatch. From above. (See photo.)

### Page 2 — "STAKEOUT LOG"

> **Week 1:** Set standard snap traps in attic. Baited with peanut butter. Checked daily. All traps sprung, bait gone. Nothing caught. Heard movement from attic while checking traps.
>
> **Week 1, cont:** Sat in upstairs hallway from 11 PM to 3 AM. Heard the "whispering" at approximately 1:30 AM. Lasted for several minutes. Could not determine direction — seemed to come from everywhere in the ceiling at once.
>
> **Week 2:** Set live trap at base of attic hatch (cage trap, borrowed from Joe Mencha). Left overnight. Trap was knocked over. Not sprung. Cage was on its side with the door still open. The bait — an entire can of tuna — was gone.
>
> **Week 2, cont:** Inspected attic with flashlight. Smell is MUCH worse. Found what I think are droppings, but couldn't get a clear look — they were deep in the insulation. Heard shifting above me while I was on the ladder. It is AWARE of my presence.
>
> **Week 3:** Sealed two gaps I found along the roofline. Activity continued that same night. Either I missed an entry point or it doesn't need one.
>
> **Week 3, cont:** Stationed myself in the kitchen overnight with the lights off. Heard something in the walls. Heard the pantry door creak. By the time I turned on my flashlight, nothing. Pantry door was open. A bag of flour had been torn. Whatever this is, it moves FAST and it moves SILENT.
>
> **Week 4:** I am running out of ideas.

---

### Page 3 — "WHAT I THINK"

> This is not an animal.
>
> I've been doing this for almost a month. I have never seen it. I have never caught it. I have never even found clear physical evidence of what it IS — no tracks, no fur, no corpse, nothing. Just the smell, the sounds, the scratches, and the food.
>
> Animals get caught in traps. Animals leave tracks. Animals make mistakes. Whatever is in this house makes NO mistakes. It avoids every trap. It knows when I'm there. It moves through walls and ceilings without making enough noise for me to locate it — but it WHISPERS. It whispers to this family at night and it is driving them to the edge.
>
> The twelve-year-old brought me pages she photocopied from a ghost-hunting book at the school library. Under normal circumstances I'd dismiss that. But the kid's description of what she's heard — the whispering, the movement patterns, the intelligence — matches what I've observed better than any animal behavior I know of.
>
> I'm beginning to think something from one of the other cases has nested in this house. The scratching, the food theft, the physical manipulation of objects (the BRICK) — this is something with hands, with intelligence, with intent.
>
> I need to go back. I need to figure out what this is.
>
> God, it's embarrassing how long this is taking me.
