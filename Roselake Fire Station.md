# Roselake Fire Station

**Type:** Emergency Services
**Location:** South end of [[Town Center]], Roselake, adjacent to the [[The Sheriff's Office|Sheriff's Office]]

---

## Description

A reddish-brown brick building with a single truck garage housing Roselake's one fire engine. Off to the left of the main garage door is a side entrance that opens into a small interior with checkered tile floors and a persistent smell of cigarette smoke. A dispatcher's desk sits near the entrance; beyond it is a back area where crew can be found.

The building next door — the Sheriff's office — is a much more tired affair: a small, white-painted wood building that looks like it's been peeling for decades.

Because Roselake has no hospital, no ambulance service, and no dedicated paramedics, the fire station covers all emergency functions not handled by the Sheriff's office. This includes medical emergencies, handled with first aid kits, good intentions, and the knowledge that the nearest hospital is hours away. The station has a radio for calling county emergency services, but response times from outside are long.

---

## Staff

**[[Fire Chief Bill]]** — The chief. Handlebar mustache, practical and gruff. Keeps the operation running with only three total staff. Was on scene the night [[Matthew Davis]] was attacked by the [[Anglerfish]] (March 14, 1981) and helped patch him up. He and some crew searched the base of [[Mount Roselake]] the following morning and found nothing. Accompanied Matthew and [[Agatha Graves]] to the attack site on March 16 for a daytime investigation.

**Shawna** — The dispatcher. Curmudgeonly, looks older than she is. Her voice carries effortlessly through the building when she needs to get someone's attention. A capable emergency medic when the situation demands it — on March 17, 1981, she reset [[Matthew Davis]]'s compound fracture and realigned his broken spine on an excellent Medicine roll, cigarette clenched between her teeth. She did not hide her anger with [[Reverend Detty]] that night, scolding him for his "wild theories" and for nearly getting Matthew killed.

Two additional staff members work shifts and are largely on-call from home. On the night of March 17, the on-duty firefighter — a clean-shaven young man of about twenty — helped stabilize and splint both [[Matthew Davis]] and [[Reverend Detty]] after their fall at the [[The Old Radio Tower|Old Radio Tower]]. He asked the Reverend not to go back up there: "We need you around this town, especially now."

---

## Relevance

The fire station is a reminder of Roselake's vulnerability. The absence of real medical infrastructure feeds directly into [[The Secondary Entities|The Unprotected]] — the pervasive, low-level fear that when something goes truly wrong, help is not coming in time.

## Investigator Visits

**[[Session 3]] — March 16, 1981:** [[Matthew Davis]] and [[Agatha Graves]] recruited [[Fire Chief Bill]] to accompany the daytime investigation at [[Mount Roselake]].

**[[Session 5]] — March 17, 1981 (~10:30–11 PM):** [[Reverend Detty]] brought in the unconscious [[Matthew Davis]] after their fall from the [[The Old Radio Tower|Old Radio Tower]]. Shawna triaged, reset Matthew's compound fracture, and realigned his spine; the on-duty firefighter splinted Detty's broken arm. The firefighter expressed open concern about the two of them going back out; Shawna expressed hers in a more acid register. Matthew remained unconscious at 2/11 HP at session end, under observation overnight.
