# Tony's Diner

**Type:** Restaurant / Social Hub
**Location:** [[Town Center]], Roselake
**Owner:** [[Tony Manitole]]

---

## Description

Tony's Diner is a squat, wood-paneled building on the main road through Roselake's town center. A neon "OPEN" sign buzzes in the window — one of the few neon signs in town. The interior is classic small-town diner: a long Formica counter with red vinyl stools, a row of booths along the windows, and a kitchen visible through a pass-through window behind the counter. The walls are decorated with faded photographs of Roselake from various decades, a mounted fish that nobody remembers catching, and a hand-lettered sign that reads "NO CHECKS — NO EXCEPTIONS — YES TONY MEANS IT."

The diner serves breakfast, lunch, and dinner — simple fare. Burgers, sandwiches, pie, and coffee that is always either fresh or about to be. Tony makes the pies himself.

---

## Role in Town

Because Roselake receives almost no through-traffic, Tony's Diner operates almost exclusively as a local hangout. It is where people come to eat, talk, complain, gossip, and spend an hour out of the house. The diner is the social center of Roselake — the place where news travels, rumors start, and information circulates.

Tony has a particular fondness for the younger crowd and lets high school kids hang around after hours. The teenagers of Roselake treat the diner as a second home.

---

## Relevance to Cases

Tony's Diner is where players will hear the most gossip about current events in Roselake. Conversations overheard at the diner can point toward any of the active cases — the Marsh family's "demon," the Hutchins family's "ghost," the Peabody house disappearances, the mayor's strange behavior, and more. Tony himself is a walking encyclopedia of town gossip, and he was one of Thomas McMullin's few regular conversation partners.
