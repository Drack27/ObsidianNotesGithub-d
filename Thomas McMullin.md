# Thomas McMullin

**Age:** Late 40s
**Residence:** The abandoned radio station atop [[Mount Roselake]] (his "lair"); previously had a small apartment in town.
**Occupation:** Former librarian at [[Roselake Academy]] / [[Roselake Library]]. Self-appointed paranormal investigator.
**Status:** Dead. Taken by [[Anglerfish|the Anglerfish]] on **March 14, 1981**, while heading up to the Old Radio Tower on Mount Roselake.
**Case Connections:** All cases. Thomas investigated every active mystery in Roselake.

---

## Appearance

A thin, wiry man with a perpetually worried expression, wire-rimmed glasses, and a habit of wearing the same corduroy jacket regardless of weather. His hands are always ink-stained from writing notes. He looks like a man who sleeps too little and reads too much — because he is.

---

## Background

Thomas McMullin was the librarian at Roselake Academy for over fifteen years. He was quiet, bookish, and deeply private — the kind of person who could spend an entire day surrounded by people and never say a word to any of them. He was not antisocial; he simply preferred books to conversation. The few people who did manage to hold regular conversations with him were **[[Tony Manitole]]** at the diner and the **[[Roselake Academy Janitorial Staff|janitorial staff]]** at the school.

Thomas had a deep fascination with conspiracy theories, which he discussed freely with Tony and the janitors — though he always insisted, adamantly and repeatedly, that he never actually *believed* any of them. They were an intellectual interest. A hobby. He found the patterns interesting.

That changed when things started happening in Roselake that no conspiracy theory could explain.

---

## The Investigation

At some point in the late 1970s, Thomas began noticing things that didn't add up. Strange disappearances. Behavioral changes in townspeople. Unexplained phenomena in homes. The Sheriff wasn't investigating. Nobody seemed to care. Thomas, whose entire professional life had been spent organizing information and finding patterns, couldn't let it go.

He began compiling case files — newspaper clippings, photographs, handwritten notes, hand-drawn maps and timelines. He organized his findings into manila folders and pinned connections on a corkboard in the basement of the abandoned radio station on Mount Roselake, which became his personal headquarters for the investigation.

Over time, Thomas identified and documented seven distinct cases:
- **[[Anglerfish Thread|The Case of the Skinwalkers]]** — people being replaced by hollow mimics
- **[[The Antimemetic Killer|The Case of the Missing Missing]]** — people being erased from memory
- **[[The Case of the Blurred Woman]]** — a ghost with an unfocusable face
- **[[Mirror Haunt|The Case of the Mirror Haunt]]** — something in Edith Zelenskyy's mirrors
- **[[Lonely Painting|The Case of the Peabody House]]** — three families vanishing from the same farmhouse
- **[[The Weeping House|The Case of the Weeping House]]** — apparent demonic infestation (actually bad pipes)
- **[[The Scratching|The Case of the Scratching]]** — something in the Hutchins family's walls (actually raccoons)

Thomas was brilliant, dedicated, and perceptive — but not infallible. Two of his seven cases turned out to be entirely mundane, and he was wrong about both of them. He spent weeks trying to catch raccoons and failed completely. He told Clara Marsh her house probably wasn't just plumbing and was starting to believe it himself. These failures are important: Thomas could be wrong, and players should think critically about everything he left behind.

---

## The Radio Tower Lair

The abandoned radio station atop Mount Roselake — formerly home to KGMR before the station moved closer to Omaha — became Thomas's base of operations. The two-story building (with a basement accessible via interior staircase or an exterior door on one side) housed his corkboard, his case files, his photographs, and his notes. It was where he retreated to think, to plan, and to try to make sense of what was happening to his town.

The basement is where the corkboard and case folders are. This is what the players will find when they arrive.

---

## Personality

Thomas was not a brave man. He was a frightened man who investigated anyway. Every one of his case files contains a note — a sticky note, an index card, a scrawled warning — about what might happen to him. He was acutely aware that whatever was happening in Roselake could happen to him too.

He was also deeply lonely, though he would never have admitted it. His reclusive nature kept people at arm's length, and his obsessive investigation consumed what social life he had. Tony at the diner was probably the closest thing he had to a friend.

---

## Death

On **March 14, 1981**, Thomas was heading up to the Old Radio Tower on Mount Roselake — his usual route, the path he'd walked hundreds of times. The Anglerfish was waiting.

Thomas was lured, bitten, and killed by the Anglerfish in the fields near the base of Mount Roselake. His body was consumed. No skin puppet was created to replace him — Thomas was too reclusive, too isolated, for a puppet to be useful. Nobody would notice a behavioral change in a man nobody talked to. The Anglerfish simply removed him.

Thomas McMullin's death occurred only hours before the first player characters arrive at the radio tower. His case files, his corkboard, his photographs, and his warnings are all still there — untouched, waiting to be found by whoever comes looking.

---

## The Contradictory Warnings

Thomas, characteristically, left "if I'm gone" warnings in multiple case folders — each one reflecting his anxiety about whichever case he was working on at the time. The warnings contradict each other, because Thomas didn't know which threat would get him. He was afraid of all of them.

The result is that players who read through his materials will find multiple, conflicting explanations for his disappearance — each one pointing at a different case. Figuring out what actually happened to Thomas is itself a mystery.

---

## Relationship with Jonathan Oesch

Thomas had a single exchange with [[Jonathan Oesch]], years before the disappearances and murder. Jonathan came into the library to check out a book on ancient occult practices. Thomas was accustomed to odd requests — but Jonathan's silence and the way he **stared daggers straight at Thomas** almost the entire time was deeply unnerving. Jonathan didn't browse. He didn't read. He just stood there holding the book and staring.

Thomas checked the book out for him without comment. Jonathan left without a word. Thomas steered clear of him ever since. He cannot articulate exactly why Jonathan unsettles him, but the feeling never faded. In his notes on the Missing Missing case, Thomas admits he needs to talk to Jonathan about Sally's disappearance — but something about that man makes every hair on the back of his neck stand up.

---

## Legacy

Thomas McMullin is the connective tissue of the entire campaign. His case files are the players' entry point into every mystery in Roselake. His observations are sharp, his documentation is thorough, and his instincts are mostly right — but he's dead now, and he can't answer questions.

The players inherit his work. What they do with it is up to them.
