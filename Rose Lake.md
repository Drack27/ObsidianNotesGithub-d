# Rose Lake

About 3 miles to the northeast of town center. It is a large, fenced-in body of water (between a lake and a pond in size) that serves primarily as a drainage ditch for local farm runoff. It earned its name "Rose Lake" after an agricultural accident involving nitrate fertilizer turned the water a murky, toxic pinkish-red.

The lake is surrounded by a tall chain-link fence topped with razor wire (hidden cleverly). Danger signs featuring a stick figure and a toxic amoeba warn people to keep out. The smell of fertilizer is prominent, particularly near the large drainage tunnels that empty into the lake.

## Intrusion Institute Monitoring Station
On the far side of the lake, furthest from the road, the fence line sweeps outwards to enclose a small nubbin of land. Built on this isolated patch of land is a tiny, squat concrete structure covered in antennas and satellite dishes.

This is a covert monitoring station run by the **[[Intrusion Institute Agents|Intrusion Institute]]**, allowing them to keep tabs on the paranormal anomalies in Roselake while remaining hidden from the townspeople.

## Investigator Visits
**[[Session 4]] — March 17, 1981 (evening):** [[Victor Hayes]] visits Rose Lake. He walks the perimeter, observes the nitrate fertilizer draining into the lake, and spots the Intrusion Institute Monitoring Station on the far side before returning to his car, shaking off the feeling of being watched.
