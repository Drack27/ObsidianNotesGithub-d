# PLAYER HANDOUT — The Case of the Skinwalkers

## GM Note: The Corkboard

_The east wall of the basement is dominated by a large corkboard, roughly 4 feet by 3 feet. Red yarn connects several photographs to a central index card reading "THE SKINWALKERS" in heavy black marker. Thomas has clearly been at this for a while — there are pushpin holes where things have been moved around, coffee ring stains on the board's frame, and a few sticky notes that have fallen to the floor. Six photographs are pinned to the board, each with an index card clipped beneath it. A thin manila folder labeled "SKINWALKERS — NOTES" sits on the desk below the board, nearly empty._

---

## The Photographs & Index Cards

_Read or relay the following descriptions to the players as they examine each photo and its attached card._

---

### PHOTO 1 — Sylvia Lemmon

**Photo description:** A candid shot taken from across the street, slightly blurry. A teenage girl with long dark hair and a denim jacket stands outside of what looks like a convenience store, mid-conversation with another teen. She looks irritated. A second photo is stapled behind the first — a cleaner image, probably pulled from a school yearbook, showing the same girl smiling in a class photo.

**Index card (handwritten, blue ink):**

> SYLVIA LEMMON — 17 — East side
> 
> Disappeared ~25 days ago. Gone for 2 days. Parents filed report, then retracted it when she came back.
> 
> BEFORE: Quiet, well-behaved. Honor roll (?). Church girl. AFTER: Hostile. Talks back. Caught smoking behind the gas station on Elm. Mom says she "doesn't even recognize her own daughter anymore." Direct quote — heard it at the diner.
> 
> Status: SWAPPED. Early case. First one I noticed.

---

### PHOTO 2 — Wendy Simons

**Photo description:** Taken through a window from outside — a woman behind a cash register at what is clearly a grocery store. She's mid-scan on an item, staring straight ahead with a flat, neutral expression. The fluorescent lighting makes the photo look washed out and clinical. She looks like she's sleepwalking.

**Index card (handwritten, blue ink, with a section underlined twice):**

> WENDY SIMONS — 32 — Cashier, Joe's Superstore
> 
> Missed work for several days. No one could reach her. Came back like nothing happened.
> 
> BEFORE: Friendly. Chatty. Knew everybody's name at the register. Regulars liked her. AFTER: ~~Monotone~~ ROBOTIC. Says the right words but there's nothing behind them. Like she's reading from a script. Scans items, bags them, says "have a nice day." Goes home. Feeds her cat. That's it. THAT'S IT.
> 
> Talked to her coworker Deb — Deb says "it's like the lights are on but nobody's home." Deb is scared of her.
> 
> Status: SWAPPED. No question.

---

### PHOTO 3 — Zacharias & Toula Carmody

**Photo description:** Two photos pinned side by side, connected by a single piece of red yarn. The first is a slightly out-of-focus shot of an older couple leaving a church, the man holding the door for the woman. Both are dressed nicely but their expressions are blank — mouths closed, eyes forward. The second photo is a clipping from what looks like a church newsletter, showing the same couple smiling broadly at a potluck dinner, arms around each other. The contrast is stark.

**Index card (handwritten, blue ink, cramped — he ran out of room):**

> ZACHARIAS "ZACH" & TOULA CARMODY — married, 60s (?)
> 
> Kids grown & moved out of state. No one to notice them go missing.
> 
> BEFORE: The most social couple at First Methodist. Zach organized the pancake breakfast every year. Toula ran the quilting circle. They LOVED to talk. Couldn't shut them up (I mean that affectionately). AFTER: Won't speak. At all. Sit in the back pew now. Leave immediately after service. Don't make eye contact. Someone on the church board tried to ask if they were okay and Toula just stared at him.
> 
> Been watching their house. Lights go on and off at the same times every night. EXACT same times. To the minute.
> 
> Status: BOTH SWAPPED. Same time? How?

---

### PHOTO 4 — Donny McPhee

**Photo description:** A man in dirty overalls and a sun-bleached cap standing next to a fence post on what looks like farmland. He's caught mid-turn, like he noticed the camera. His face is deeply lined and unfriendly. One hand grips the fence post hard. His posture is slightly off — leaning to one side.

**Index card (handwritten, blue ink):**

> DONNY McPHEE — Farmer — north county road
>
> No disappearance that I know of, but sudden personality shift. About 2 weeks ago.
>
> BEFORE: Gruff but decent. Waved at people. Came into town for supplies Saturdays. Kept to himself but wasn't HOSTILE. AFTER: Mean. Snapped at Linda at the hardware store — she said he practically bit her head off over a bag of nails. Hasn't been to the diner in 2 weeks — Earl says he came in once, ordered coffee, didn't talk to anybody, and left. Walks different — there's a limp he's trying to hide. Favors his left leg.
>
> Haven't gotten close enough to confirm. Hard to surveil — open farmland, no cover. Drove past his property twice. Second time he was on his porch and STARED at me until I was out of sight.
>
> Status: LIKELY SWAPPED. Need more info.

---

### PHOTO 5 — Winfred (Dog)

**Photo description:** A slightly awkward photo of a medium-sized dog — some kind of hound mix — lying on a front porch. The dog's legs are stiff and extended and it stares at the camera with a dull, glassy expression. A second photo behind it shows the same dog from what looks like happier times, mid-play with a tennis ball, ears up, mouth open.

**Index card (handwritten, blue ink, with a question mark circled several times in the margin):**

> "WINFRED" — Dog — belongs to the Hubbard family, Maple St.
> > 
> But the Hubbards say he changed overnight. Used to be energetic, playful, dumb in a sweet way. Now he barely moves. Stiff. Moves like his joints are made of wood. Stares at nothing.
> 
> If they can swap people, why not animals?
> 
> Status: POSSIBLY SWAPPED. 

---

### PHOTO 6 — Mayor Lance Winnipeg

**Photo description:** The clearest and most recent-looking photo on the board. A man in a slightly rumpled button-down shirt, mid-gesture, apparently talking loudly to someone off-camera outside what looks like Town Hall. His mouth is wide open and his body language is aggressive — leaning forward, one hand jabbing the air. A second photo, clipped from what looks like a local newspaper, shows the same man shaking someone's hand at a ribbon cutting, smiling warmly.

**Index card (handwritten, blue ink — the handwriting is noticeably more agitated here, pressing harder):**

> MAYOR LANCE WINNIPEG — 37
> 
> THIS IS THE BIG ONE.
> 
> Before: Kindest man in town. Looked out for everyone. Soft-spoken. Thoughtful. The kind of guy who remembers your kid's name. After: LOUD. Impulsive. Got into a shouting match with a council member IN PUBLIC last Tuesday. Made a snap decision about the water treatment budget that nobody understood. Doesn't seem to think before he speaks anymore.
> 
> If they've gotten to the MAYOR, how far along is this?
> 
> People are noticing. EVERYONE is noticing.
> 
> Status: SWAPPED. GOD HELP US.

## The Manila Folder

_The folder is thin. Inside, the players find:_

**A single sheet of lined notebook paper with a handwritten list titled "PATTERN?":**

> What they have in common:
> 
> 1. Brief disappearance or unexplained absence (not always — McPhee? Winfred?)
> 2. Personality change — always a REDUCTION. Less social. Less emotional. Less human.
> 3. Nobody can explain it
> 4. Nobody is doing anything about it
> 
> What I don't know:
> 
> 1. How are they being taken?
> 2. Where are they being taken?
> 3. What happens to the real person?
> 4. What ARE the replacements?
> 5. How many more are there that I haven't noticed?
> 6. Is there a pattern to WHO gets targeted?

**A torn-out newspaper clipping** from the Roselake Register, dated about 3 weeks ago. It's a short community interest piece about Mayor Winnipeg's "new energy" at the latest town council meeting. The reporter's tone is positive but slightly confused. Thomas has circled the phrase "uncharacteristically animated" and written **"UNDERSTATEMENT"** in the margin.

**A sticky note, stuck to the inside of the folder:**

> If I go missing — if I "come back" different — don't trust it. Don't trust me. — T.M.
