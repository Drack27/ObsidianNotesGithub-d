


## Explicitly Named Locations

> [!info]- Roselake, Nebraska
> A highly isolated, dead-end agricultural town suffering from a pervasive, oppressive atmosphere of dread. The town lacks basic infrastructure, notably a hospital, which deeply fuels the local populace's underlying fears of isolation, ignorance, and failure.
> [[Roselake, Nebraska|View Roselake, Nebraska's full details here]]

> [!info]- Mount Roselake
> The local nickname for the town's highest hill, which is only maybe 10 feet higher than all the other rolling hills in the area
> [[Mount Roselake|View Mount Roselake's full details here]]

> [!info]- The Old Radio Tower & Abandoned Radio Station
> Situated on mount . The abandoned station at the top served as the personal lair for Thomas McMullin before his demise or disappearance.
> [[The Old Radio Tower|View The Old Radio Tower's full details here]]

> [!info]- Tony's Diner
> A local diner situated by the road in town. Because the town receives almost no through-traffic, it operates almost exclusively as a local hangout. It is a favored gathering spot for the younger residents of Roselake.
> [[Tony's Diner|View Tony's Diner's full details here]]

> [!info]- Roselake Academy
> The town's educational facility. Due to the town's small size and budget, it also houses the public library.
> [[Roselake High School|View Roselake High School's full details here]]

> [!info]- Roselake Library
> A public facility bolted onto Roselake High School. It was formerly managed by Thomas McMullin. There are ominous hints that something unnatural might have taken up residence within its walls.
> [[Roselake Library|View Roselake Library's full details here]]

> [!info]- Castle Roselake
> The somewhat mocking, cute local nickname for the municipal building that houses both the Post Office and the Mayor's Office.
> [[Castle Roselake|View Castle Roselake's full details here]]

> [!info]- The Convenience / Video Rental Store
> A strange hybrid business. It functions primarily as a 7-Eleven-style convenience store selling snacks and gas station goods, with a Blockbuster-style video rental store bolted onto the back.
> [[The Convenience / Video Rental Store|View The Convenience / Video Rental Store's full details here]]

> [!info]- Joe's Superstore.
> The biggest building in Roselake A standard retail location providing basic goods and supplies to the town's residents.
> [[Joe's Superstore|View The General Store's full details here]]

> [!info]- The Sheriff's Office
> The headquarters for local law enforcement.
> [[The Sheriff's Office|View The Sheriff's Office's full details here]]

> [!info]- Rose Lake
> The town's namesake, which is actually just a semi-natural drainage ditch functioning as a mini endorheic basin for local agricultural runoff. It is fenced off with chain-link and heavily avoided by locals due to a nasty pink film caused by an old fertilizer spill and stagnant algae blooms.
> [[Rose Lake|View Rose Lake's full details here]]

> [!info]- Intrusion Institute Monitoring Station
> A covert, tiny outpost operated by a global paranormal containment organization. It uses the chain-link fence and the toxic reputation of Rose Lake as a cover to secretly monitor the escalating Entity activity in the area.
> [[Intrusion Institute Monitoring Station|View Intrusion Institute Monitoring Station's full details here]]

> [!info]- Roselake Fire Station
> Covers not just fire, but basically all emergency functions not covered by the Sheriff's office, including medical emergencies. 
> [[Roselake Fire Station|View Intrusion Institute Monitoring Station's full details here]]
## Implied Locations

> [!info]- The Fields
> The expansive, lonely agricultural tracts surrounding the town. They are implied to be the hunting grounds for a horror spawned by "The Isolation" that lures people into the dark.
