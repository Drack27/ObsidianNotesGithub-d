## Part 5: Active Cases in Early 1981

### THE CASE OF THE BLURRED WOMAN — Escalating Manifestations (~1978–March 1981)

- **Entity:** The ghost of Margaret Anne Hollowell (1847–1889), supernaturally sustained by The Forgotten.
- **Appearance:** Woman in 1800s dress, thin frame, normal height, rigid posture. Her face is BLURRED — not hidden but smeared, impossible to focus on. She does not speak or attack. She stands, watches, and disappears.
- **Trigger Condition:** Appears in homes with a high localized concentration of athazagoraphobia (fear of being forgotten).
- **Desire:** To be REMEMBERED — to be seen and acknowledged.

#### Known Sightings (increasing in frequency):
1. **Janet & Ray Pruitt** — single sighting in their home.
2. **Gus Brennan** — THREE sightings (the only repeat witness):
   - Once in his kitchen
   - Once at the foot of his bed
   - Once on his back porch
   - Gus's reaction: sad rather than frightened. Describes her as "someone waiting to be picked up who knows nobody's coming." Says: *"She looks like she wants something. But I can't tell what, because I can't see her damn face."*
3. **Dolores Fenton** — single sighting.
4. **Vacant Wakefield house** — sighting reported.
5. **Thomas McMullin** — single sighting.

#### Key Danger:
- The Blurred Woman is caught between wanting to be remembered and being trapped by The Forgotten.
- If antagonized sufficiently, she could become a fully willing Avatar of The Forgotten — a catastrophic escalation.

#### Resolution Path:
- Discover her identity, birth/death dates, and at least one personal fact about her life.
- Speak these aloud while she manifests to resolve the blurring and lay her to rest.

### THE ANGLERFISH — Skinwalker Replacements (Active, March 1981)

- **Entity:** Anglerfish-like creature spawned from the commingling of **The Uncanny** and **The Predator** (two Great Entities).
- **Method:** Kills victims and replaces them with "skin puppets" — hollow replicas filled with black-gray ashy/powdery substance (sawdust-like).
- **Puppet Properties:**
  - Passable but imperfect mimics
  - Cannot learn anything new; operate only on the victim's existing knowledge
  - Very solid when struck
  - If cut open: filled with black-gray ash
  - People around them sense something has changed but can't pinpoint what

#### Confirmed Replacements:
1. **Wendy Simons** (32) — cashier at Joe's Superstore. Now exhibits monotone, robotic behavior.
2. **Zach & Toula Carmody** (married, 60s) — both replaced simultaneously. No longer speak at church.

#### False Leads (NOT replaced):
- **Sylvia Lemmon** (17) — went on a road trip, came back rebellious. Normal teenage behavior.
- **Donny McPhee** (farmer) — broke his leg, hiding the injury. Pain makes him grouchy. Not a replacement.
- **Winfred** (dog) — suffers from joint disease. Not a replacement.

#### Future Target (not yet bitten):
- **Mayor Lance Winnipeg** (37) — likely to be lured and bitten within weeks if nothing intervenes.

### THE MIRROR HAUNT — Edith Zelenskyy's Haunting (Active, ~January–March 1981)

- **Entity:** Mirror Haunt — a spawn of **The Eye** (secondary entity representing fear of being watched).
- **Appearance:** Extremely skinny, gaunt humanoid in a plain bluish off-white nightgown. Giant round smooth featureless head with only lips visible when mouth closed. When mouth opens: a single giant eyeball fills the entire head interior (no skull; skin is a thin membrane).
- **Victim:** Edith Zelenskyy (late 60s/early 70s), a widower living alone in a small house on the east edge of town past the grain elevator.
- **Why Edith:** Russian immigrant granddaughter; spent time homeless in Omaha earlier in life. This trauma left her hypervigilant, paranoid, and deeply fearful of losing her home — a perfect fear signature for The Eye.

#### Escalation Stages:
| Stage | Description | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Stage 1 — Presence | Victim feels watched near reflective surfaces; nothing visible | Passed |
| Stage 2 — Glimpses | Brief peripheral glimpses; nothing when looking directly | Passed |
| **Stage 3 — Manifestation** | **Fully visible in reflections; stands behind/beside victim with mouth open, eye staring; NOT in physical space, only in reflections** | **CURRENT** |
| Stage 4 — Emergence | Steps out of reflection into physical world; follows target everywhere; conceals itself from all but the target | Weeks away |

#### Observable Signs:
- Withdrawn from all public life
- All curtains drawn; yard overgrown
- Every reflective surface in the house has been covered, painted, or removed
- Truck mirrors wrapped
- Bathroom window painted over
- Haggard appearance, dark circles, jumpy, avoids eye contact
- Shops at odd hours to avoid people
- Neighbor Margaret Hale has heard shouting inside (Edith lives alone)
- Neighbor has left supplies on the porch
- Thomas McMullin visited 3 times; Edith told him to leave at least once

#### Critical Detail:
- Edith refuses to seek help because she is terrified that admitting her situation will result in people taking her home away — sending her to a hospital or back to the street.
- If the Mirror Haunt reaches Stage 4 and Edith's fear reaches zero (complete psychological breakdown), it merges into a **Disciple of The Eye**.
