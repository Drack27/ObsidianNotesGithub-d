# The Case of the Mirror Haunt

# PART ONE — Thomas's File

_What Thomas McMullin has gathered. This is what players/readers would encounter if they looked through his materials._

## Thomas's Theory

Thomas has been hearing rumors about Edith Zelenskyy — an elderly woman who lives alone in a small house on the edge of town. Neighbors have noticed her curtains are always drawn now. She's stopped coming into town for groceries as often as she used to. When she does come in, she looks haggard — dark circles, jumpy, won't make eye contact. A couple of people have tried to ask if she's alright and she brushes them off.

Thomas visited her property once and noticed that every mirror-like surface visible from outside — the bathroom window, the side mirror on her truck — had been covered or turned away. He knocked on her door. She told him to leave.

Thomas believes something in her house is terrorizing her, and that she's too stubborn or too scared to ask for help. He doesn't know what it is. He suspects a haunting — something that uses reflective surfaces.

## What Thomas Has Observed

- Every reflective surface he can see from the exterior has been covered, taped over, or turned around. The bathroom window has been painted over from the inside.
- Edith used to keep a tidy yard. It's overgrown now.
- She still goes to the general store for supplies, but comes at odd hours — early morning, right before closing. Avoids people.
- Thomas spoke briefly with Edith's neighbor, **Margaret Hale**, who said Edith told her "I can't have anyone in the house right now" and wouldn't explain further.
- Margaret also mentioned hearing Edith talking loudly — possibly shouting — inside her house at night, though Edith lives alone.

## What Thomas Doesn't Know

Thomas hasn't been able to get Edith to talk. He doesn't know what she's seeing, what the entity looks like, how it behaves, or why it chose her. He only knows the external signs: the covered mirrors, the isolation, the fear.

# PART TWO — What's Actually Happening

_The full truth. Thomas does not have access to any of this information._

## The Mirror Haunt

An entity that inhabits reflective surfaces. It is drawn to isolated individuals and feeds on the fear of being watched — the creeping, intimate violation of never being alone, even in your own home.

### Appearance

A nondescript, extremely skinny, gaunt humanoid shape wearing a plain bluish off-white nightgown. Its head is a single giant round orb with no features except a pair of lips that span almost the entirety of the front of the head. When the mouth is closed, the head is a smooth, featureless sphere with only the seam of the lips visible.

When the mouth opens, it reveals teeth framing a **single giant eyeball** that occupies the entirety of the interior of the head. The veins of the eye are visible shifting through the skin — the creature has no skull. The skin of the head is essentially a thin membrane stretched over the eye.

**The feeling of being watched only begins when the mouth is open.** The creature can be present in a mirror with its mouth closed and the victim will feel nothing. The watching is the point.

### Behavior & Escalation

The Mirror Haunt follows a slow escalation pattern:

**Stage 1 — Presence.** The victim begins to feel watched when near reflective surfaces. Nothing is visible. Just the prickling sensation of being observed. This can last days or weeks.

**Stage 2 — Glimpses.** The victim begins to catch brief, peripheral glimpses of something in mirrors — a shape that shouldn't be there, a flicker of movement. When they look directly, nothing. This stage erodes sleep and trust in one's own perception.

**Stage 3 — Manifestation.** The Mirror Haunt becomes fully visible in reflective surfaces. It stands behind or beside the victim in the reflection, mouth open, eye staring. It does not appear in the physical space — only in the reflection. The victim can see it watching them, but cannot interact with it or escape its gaze except by removing or covering all reflective surfaces.

**Stage 4 — (Unknown/TBD).** What happens if the escalation is allowed to continue unchecked has not been established.

## Edith Zelenskyy

**Age:** Late 60s to early 70s.

**Background:** Granddaughter of Russian immigrants. Lives alone in a small house on the edge of town. Spent time on the streets in Omaha earlier in her life — she was homeless for a period and it left deep scars. She fears, above almost anything, being forced to leave her home again. The streets taught her that losing your home means losing everything.

**Current situation:** Edith is at Stage 3 of the Mirror Haunt's escalation. She can see the entity clearly in any reflective surface. She has covered or destroyed every mirror in her house. She has painted over the bathroom window. She avoids water when it's still enough to reflect.

**Why she won't tell anyone:** Edith is terrified that if she tells people what she's seeing, they will think she's lost her mind and try to remove her from her home — either into a hospital or onto the street. She would rather live with the thing in the mirrors than risk losing the only safe place she has. She is angry, frightened, and deeply stubborn.

She shouts at the entity at night. She refuses to be passive about it. But she's losing the war of attrition — she's exhausted, sleep-deprived, and running out of surfaces to cover.
