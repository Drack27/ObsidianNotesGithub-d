# Marcus Vale

**Age:** Unknown (adult)
**Occupation:** Computer programmer / government contractor
**Status:** Active player character. Introduced [[Session 7]].

---

## Appearance

A Black man of average height — around 5'9", lean build (described as programmer-physique — he could probably hit the gym, but hasn't). Wavy hair, kept neat. An impressive thick mustache that covers most of his upper lip. His look is put-together without being flashy.

---

## Background

Marcus Vale is a computer programmer with government contractor experience — the kind of person who gets called in when an operation needs someone who can make hardware talk, pull data off old systems, or figure out what a piece of technology actually is. He has worked with the military at times, which required clearance. He and [[Victor Hayes]] know each other through government channels; when Victor hit a wall with the electronic equipment recovered in Roselake, he called Marcus in.

Marcus arrived in Roselake toward the end of March 1981. He is staying in the attic room of **Francine and Tom**, an older farm couple who are happy to have the extra income from a boarder.

What Marcus makes of Roselake — the quiet, the tension, the slow accumulation of strangeness — has not yet been fully established. He came for the hardware. He seems like someone who stays adaptable.

---

## Arrival and First Work (Late March – April 3, 1981)

On arrival, Victor gave Marcus the mystery machine (a broken VHS player) and two gunky VHS tapes recovered from a dumpster behind [[The Convenience and Video Rental Store|Charlie's Quick Stop]], along with the case files and documentation. Marcus cleaned and repaired the VHS player using parts sourced from [[Charlie Haverford]] at the Quick Stop.

He screened both gunky VHS tapes:

- One contained the 1979 film ***All That Jazz***
- One contained a martial arts film, labeled *Enter the Dragon*

Neither was useful to the investigation.

The three **reel-to-reel tapes** from the radio tower (dated 1961, 1955, and 1950) could not be played — no equipment available in Roselake handles them.

---

## Session 7: April 4, 1981

Marcus accompanied Victor on his first full day of active investigation. They visited [[The Convenience and Video Rental Store|Charlie's Quick Stop]], [[Tony's Diner]], and the [[Roselake Library]] attached to [[Roselake Academy]].

At the library, Marcus gained access to the school's computer terminal after waiting for a child to vacate it — failed intimidation, succeeded in patience. The terminal turned out to be a standalone educational machine: no network, no useful data, only typing games and an ASCII encyclopedia. Marcus confirmed it was not connected to any external system.
