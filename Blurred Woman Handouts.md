## Left Side — Interview Cards

_The left pocket contains five index cards, each one documenting a conversation Thomas had with a witness. They're written in a hurried hand — blue ink, some words abbreviated._

---

### CARD 1 — Janet Pruitt

> **JANET PRUITT — Birch Street** Husband: Ray. Two kids (young).
> 
> Woke up ~3 AM. Woman standing in the hallway outside the kids' rooms. Old-fashioned dress — Janet said "like something from a history book." Long, to the floor.
> 
> Could NOT describe the face. Said it was "like trying to read through wax paper." Clear body, clear hands, clear dress. Face = smeared.
> 
> Janet screamed. Ray came running. Woman gone. No sign of entry. Doors locked. Windows locked.
> 
> Hasn't seen her since. Sleeps with the hallway light on. Kids didn't wake up.

---

### CARD 2 — Ray Pruitt (Follow-Up)

> **RAY PRUITT — Follow-up**
> 
> Didn't see it. Got to the hallway and Janet was standing there alone, shaking.
> 
> Says he believes Janet — she's "not the type to make things up." Checked the house top to bottom. Nothing.
> 
> Mentioned that Janet has been anxious since — checking on the kids more often. Doesn't like being alone in the house.

---

### CARD 3 — Gus Brennan

> **GUS BRENNAN — Mill Road — Widower, 70s**
> 
> Has seen her THREE TIMES. Only repeat witness.
> 
> 1st: Kitchen. Early morning. Turned around and she was standing by the stove. Stared at him. He stared back. She was gone when he blinked.
> 
> 2nd: Foot of his bed. Middle of the night. Woke up and she was just standing there. He said he lay there for "a good five minutes" watching her before she faded.
> 
> 3rd: Back porch. Dusk. She was looking in through the screen door. He walked toward her. She didn't move. He got within three feet and she was gone.
> 
> Gus's take: "She's not trying to scare me. She looks like she wants something. But I can't tell what, because I can't see her damn face."
> 
> He's not scared. He seems more sad about it than anything. Said she reminds him of "someone waiting to be picked up who knows nobody's coming."
> 
> NOTE: Gus lives alone. Wife died 4 years ago. Semi-reclusive. Fits a profile?

---

### CARD 4 — Dolores Fenton

> **DOLORES FENTON — Church Street**
> 
> Came home from church potluck. Woman was standing in her living room, FACING THE MANTELPIECE. Specifically facing the family photographs.
> 
> Woman turned toward Dolores. Dolores saw the face — or the lack of it — and fainted. Woke up on the floor. Woman was gone. Nothing disturbed. Nothing taken.
> 
> Dolores has since put all the family photos in a drawer. She didn't explain why — I think the woman looking at the photos bothered her more than the woman being there at all.
> 
> Dolores is in her 60s. Lives alone. Husband passed years ago. Active in the church but doesn't have close friends outside of it.

---

### CARD 5 — Thomas's Own Sighting

> **MY OWN — Wakefield House, Oak Avenue (VACANT)**
> 
> Walked past at dusk. Movement in the front window. Woman standing inside, perfectly still, facing the window. Facing ME.
> 
> Old dress. Thin frame. And that face — or where the face should be. Like someone smeared Vaseline on a window and put a person behind it. Everything else was clear. Just the face.
> 
> Took a photo. Too dark. Useless.
> 
> Went back the next morning. Front door locked. Dust on the floors undisturbed. No footprints. No sign anyone had been inside in months.

## Right Side — Thomas's Summary Notes

_Two pages of lined notebook paper._

---

### Page 1 — "THE BLURRED WOMAN — SUMMARY"

> Four separate households. Plus my own sighting. All describing the same figure:
> 
> — Woman in old-fashioned dress, 1800s style. Long, full-length. Dark or muted color. — Thin build. Normal height. Posture is still, almost rigid. — Face is BLURRED. Not hidden, not turned away — blurred. Every witness uses different words but describes the same thing. Wax paper. Fogged glass. Smeared. Like trying to focus on something your eyes refuse to resolve. — She does not speak. She does not attack. She stands. She watches. She disappears.
> 
> She's appeared in occupied homes (Pruitts, Gus, Dolores) and at least one vacant house (Wakefield). No pattern to the locations that I can see. Different neighborhoods. Different types of houses.
> 
> She appears to be a ghost. An old one.

---

### Page 2 — "QUESTIONS"

> Who is she? A woman from the 1800s — Roselake has been here since the 1840s. Could be anyone. Could check county records, old church registries, cemetery plots. But "woman in an old dress" isn't much to go on.
> 
> Why these houses? The Pruitts are a young family. Gus is an old widower. Dolores is a churchgoing widow. The Wakefield house is empty. I can't find the thread.
> 
> Unless... most of these people are alone. Or at least, they spend a lot of time alone. The Pruitts are the exception, but Janet saw her at 3 AM — the loneliest hour.
> 
> What does she want? Gus said she seems like she wants something. Dolores found her looking at family photos. She showed herself to me through a window.
> 
> She wants to be SEEN. That's my gut. She wants someone to look at her and know who she is.
> 
> But nobody can, because nobody can see her face.
> 
> That's the saddest damn haunting I've ever heard of.
