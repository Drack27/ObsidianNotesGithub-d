# Edith Zelenskyy

**Age:** Late 60s to early 70s
**Residence:** A small house on the eastern edge of town, past the grain elevator.
**Occupation:** Retired.
**Case Connection:** [[Mirror Haunt]]

---

## Description

Edith Zelenskyy is a thin, sharp-eyed woman with silver hair pulled back in a tight bun and the weathered hands of someone who has done hard work her entire life. She is the granddaughter of Russian immigrants who settled in the area generations ago. She has lived in Roselake for decades and has no intention of leaving — her house is the one fixed point in a life that has had too few of them.

---

## Background

Edith spent time on the streets in Omaha earlier in her life. She was homeless for a period — the details of which she does not discuss — and it left deep scars. She fears, above almost anything, being forced to leave her home again. The streets taught her that losing your home means losing everything: your safety, your dignity, your identity. She will endure almost anything to avoid that happening again.

This history left her hypervigilant. She checks the locks multiple times. She watches the street from behind her curtains. She is acutely aware of being observed, of being judged, of being found wanting. She is the kind of person who always feels like someone is watching her — which is exactly the baseline fear that [[Mirror Haunt (creature)|the Mirror Haunt]] feeds on.

---

## Current State

Edith is at **Stage 3** of the Mirror Haunt's escalation. She can see the entity clearly in any reflective surface — a gaunt humanoid shape with a featureless head and a single enormous eye. She has covered or destroyed every mirror in her house. She has painted over the bathroom window. She avoids still water. She has wrapped her truck's mirrors in tape.

She will not tell anyone what she is seeing, because she is terrified that if people think she has lost her mind, they will try to remove her from her home. She would rather live with the thing in the mirrors than risk losing the only safe place she has.

She shouts at it at night. She refuses to be passive. But she is losing — exhausted, sleep-deprived, and running out of surfaces to cover.

---

## Personality

Edith is angry, frightened, and deeply stubborn. She does not want help. She does not want pity. She wants to be left alone in her house with her fear and her fury. Getting through to her requires patience, respect, and an understanding that her resistance is not rudeness — it is survival.

If someone earns her trust — truly earns it, not just talks their way in — she will be honest about what she is experiencing. But trust is not something Edith gives easily or quickly.
