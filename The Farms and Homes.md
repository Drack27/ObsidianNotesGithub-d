# The Farms and Homes

**Type:** Residential / Agricultural
**Location:** Spread across the rolling hills surrounding [[Town Center]]

---

## Description

The farms and homes of Roselake make up the vast majority of the town's physical footprint. The town center is a small cluster of buildings — the diner, the school, the store, the municipal building — but everything else is farmland: acres and acres of lazily rolling hills planted with corn, wheat, and sorghum, dotted with farmhouses, barns, grain silos, and the occasional windmill.

The houses range from well-maintained family homesteads to sagging structures that have been slowly losing their battle with Nebraska weather for decades. Most families have lived on their land for generations. The farming community is tight-knit in the way that communities are when they depend on each other for survival — they lend equipment, share labor during harvest, and keep an eye on each other's properties. But tight-knit is not the same as close. Many of these people see their nearest neighbor only when they need something.

The isolation of the farms — the miles of empty field between houses, the long drives into town, the nights with nothing but wind and darkness — is a primary driver of [[The Secondary Entities|The Isolation]]'s power in Roselake.
