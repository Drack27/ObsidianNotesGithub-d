# Session 3

**Date (in-game):** March 16, 1981, morning through late evening
**Player Characters:** Matthew Davis and Agatha Graves
**Players:** Tucker (Matthew) and Abbie (Agatha)

---

## Events

### Meeting at Agatha's Shop

On the second day of his recovery from the [[Anglerfish]] attack, [[Matthew Davis]] limped his way to [[Agatha Graves]]'s shop — a cluttered, organized-chaos space full of taxidermy specimens, crystals, paperwork, and curiosities picked up from estate sales and local collectors. Matthew had worked with Agatha before on his academic research and knew she was the right person to talk to.

The two had never spoken about the Ghost Hunters Club flyers, but they had both responded to them. Matthew had arrived on the right night; Agatha, a day late. Both had found the basement of the [[The Old Radio Tower|Old Radio Tower]] and reviewed [[Thomas McMullin]]'s case files.

Agatha produced the large enameled tooth she had found at the base of [[Mount Roselake]] during [[Session 2]]. Matthew compared it to the puncture wounds on his back. The dimensions matched: the tooth almost certainly came from whatever attacked him.

They resolved to go back to the hill and investigate further. Matthew mentioned he had a revolver — he'd fired it at the creature during the attack and missed. They agreed they should try to get better weapons before heading out.

### Joe's Superstore

Agatha drove them in her hearse to [[Joe's Superstore]], the largest building in Roselake — a squat, brightly lit general-purpose store with long straight aisles and a neon sign above the entrance.

[[Joe Mencha]], the owner, was walking the floor greeting customers with his usual booming energy. A small family was laughing with him near the doors on their way out.

Behind them, at checkout lane seven, was a woman operating the register in a way that drew the eye: staring straight ahead, one hand moving items across the scanner in a mechanical, rote rhythm, not responding to the customer she was serving or to anything happening around her. People nearby gave her a wide berth. Both Matthew and Agatha recognized her — she was one of the subjects documented on Thomas McMullin's Skinwalkers corkboard.

Matthew asked Joe about hunting knives. Joe led him to the farm and equipment half of the store and, with an improbably good luck roll, produced something unusual from beneath the shelving: a large blade in an intricate leather-and-bead scabbard, approximately three to four feet long. Joe explained that an older local had sold it to him as a family heirloom, and he hadn't expected to move it. Matthew bought it for $200. They left with the machete and headed south.

### Ned's Farm

A short drive south of town brought them to a small farmhouse with a red barn — the property of a farmer named Ned. He was working on a tractor in the front yard when the hearse pulled up: a slightly overweight, perpetually sunburned man with sandy blonde hair and a broad-rimmed hat.

Matthew explained that he'd been attacked by an animal near the radio tower and was planning to go back to try to find it. He asked to borrow Ned's gun for a couple of days. Ned declined — he only had the one, and he'd rather hold onto it. He suggested they contact the fire department instead.

### Roselake Fire Station

Back in the civic center, the fire station sat next to the Sheriff's office. The Sheriff's building was a tired, white-painted wood affair. The fire station itself was a sturdier reddish-brown brick structure with a single truck garage. Inside, past a side door, were checkered tile floors and the smell of cigarette smoke.

The dispatcher — a curmudgeonly woman who looked older than she probably was — called back for the chief. [[Fire Chief Bill]] emerged: handlebar mustache, gear slung over his arm. He recognized Matthew from the night of the attack, and recognized Agatha by reputation.

Bill mentioned that he and some of his crew had gone back to the base of Mount Roselake the morning after the attack — March 15 — and searched. They hadn't found anything.

Matthew explained that he and Agatha were planning to go back to the hill and asked if Bill would accompany them. He made his case on appearance alone — the visible bandages, the limp, the obvious seriousness of the injuries — and Bill agreed. He arranged coverage with his two remaining staff and met them at the hearse.

### Daytime Investigation at Mount Roselake

The four miles out to the hill took only a few minutes. Under a partly cloudy March sky with a slight chill in the air, the three of them stepped out at the base of the hill.

Matthew led the way. Using his archaeological instincts — reading disturbance in the ground and vegetation the way he would read evidence at a dig — he located the attack site on a hard Archaeology success: a patch of trampled, smashed grass with old dried blood still visible.

He pressed further, attempting to read the creature's tracks. It required luck to find them without stepping on them, but there they were: **a spherical indentation in the ground, with a fan-shaped arrangement on one half.** The tracks were spaced unnaturally far apart — the spacing implied a speed that made the hairs stand up. Matthew brought Bill over to look. Bill was skeptical that these were animal prints, but deferred to Matthew's expertise.

Matthew tried to interest Bill in a nighttime stakeout — his read was that this creature probably hunted at night. Bill considered it and declined. He had three total staff and a town to watch over; if an emergency came in and he was up on a hill chasing something in the dark, there was nobody left. They drove back to town and dropped Bill off around midday.

### Evening Stakeout

As darkness fell, Matthew and Agatha returned to the hill. The night was cloudy — almost no stars — and out at the base of Mount Roselake, miles from any other building, the dark was very complete. The grasses and distant corn were a constant ocean of sound in the wind.

Both swept the area with their eyes as they hiked up (Matthew fumbled Spot Hidden; Agatha got a regular success). Neither heard anything notable. They made it to the top without incident, Matthew using his flashlight to guide them.

**Top floor of the station:** Unchanged from their respective earlier visits. Chips of concrete flaking from the walls, the broken On-Air sign hanging off to one side, the massive rusted radio equipment, cobwebs thick in the corners. The lights below were still on — Agatha had left them burning from [[Session 2]] and neither had switched them off. No signs of animal presence.

**The basement:** Thomas's desk, chair, typewriter, lamp, folders, and corkboard all in place. Matthew scanned the space carefully (hard Intelligence success) and noted that the manila folders on the desk had been shifted around since his previous visit. He asked Agatha whether she had touched them when she was here.

She confirmed she had — she had gone through each folder and tried to put everything back. The positions didn't quite match her memory of how she'd left things. The explanation was straightforward: she hadn't restored them perfectly. No one else had been in the station.

Matthew also described his pre-attack memory from the night of March 14: before the creature emerged, he had seen someone he recognized — a name he couldn't hold onto — who spoke in a flat, mechanical way, like a robot, and had said something about the Roselake Ghost Club meeting before vanishing into the brush. This was the last thing that had happened before the attack.

**Back at the attack site:** Matthew attempted to track the creature's exit path from the circular mess of prints and failed. He and Agatha then kept watch from approximately 9:20 PM to 11:00 PM. The first hour was taut and anxious. The second turned to boredom. By eleven o'clock, with no sign of the creature, they agreed to try again the next day and headed back into town.

---

## Significance

This session brought [[Matthew Davis]] and [[Agatha Graves]] together as an active investigative team. The Anglerfish tooth was confirmed as a match for Matthew's wounds — physical evidence of the creature's nature. The creature's tracks were identified for the first time: spherical indentations with a fan-shaped arrangement on one half, spaced unnaturally far apart.

[[Fire Chief Bill]] is now aware that something unusual attacked a townsperson near Mount Roselake, though he does not yet know the full scope of what is occurring.

The evening's biggest apparent discovery — that the station files had been touched — turned out to be explicable: Agatha had handled and imperfectly re-shelved them during her own earlier visit.

The creature did not appear during the nighttime stakeout. The investigation continues.
