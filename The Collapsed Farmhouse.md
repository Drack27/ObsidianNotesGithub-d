# The Collapsed Farmhouse

**Type:** Abandoned Structure
**Location:** Outside of town, off the main road
**Case Connection:** [[The Antimemetic Killer]]

---

## Description

A long-abandoned farmhouse several miles outside the Roselake town center, set back from the road at the end of an overgrown dirt track. The structure has been deteriorating for decades — the roof has partially caved in, the porch has collapsed, and the windows are either missing or opaque with grime. It is the kind of place that locals drive past without a second glance. Nobody has lived here in living memory, and nobody has any reason to visit.

The farmhouse itself is unremarkable. What matters is what's beneath it.

---

## The Underground Storeroom

Beneath the farmhouse is an **underground storeroom** — a root cellar or cold storage space that was common in farmhouses of this era. The entrance is inside the farmhouse, hidden under debris from the collapsed section of the floor. A wooden trapdoor, warped and dirt-encrusted, opens onto a narrow set of stone steps descending into a low-ceilinged chamber roughly ten feet by twelve feet, with walls of packed earth reinforced with old timber.

This is where **[[Jonathan Oesch]]** conducts his rituals.

The storeroom contains:
- The remains of completed Catalyst rituals — circles drawn in chalk and charcoal on the packed earth floor, with Latin epithets to [[The Forgotten]] inscribed around photographs of the victims.
- Animal remains in varying states of decomposition.
- A small collection of candles, chalk, and other ritual supplies stored in a rusted tin box.
- A pervasive, unpleasant smell — part decay, part damp earth, part something harder to identify.

---

## Secrecy

The only person who knows the storeroom exists is Jonathan Oesch — and even he only remembers it intermittently. The Catalyst's requirement that the completed ritual be left somewhere forgettable means that Jonathan's own memory of this place fades between visits. He maintains awareness of it only because of a **note on the reminder clock by his bedside table**, which prompts him to visit approximately once a week.

When Jonathan arrives, he remembers. When he leaves, the memory fades again. This cycle has kept the storeroom hidden from everyone, including — most of the time — its only user.

No one else in Roselake has any reason to visit the collapsed farmhouse. It is not on any path, not near any active farmland, and not visible from the road. If players find it, it will be through deliberate investigation, not accident.

---

## What Players Might Find

If the players investigate the collapsed farmhouse:
- The exterior looks like any abandoned structure — unremarkable and decaying.
- The trapdoor is not immediately obvious; it is buried under collapsed timbers and debris.
- The storeroom below contains clear evidence of ritual activity — the chalk circles, the photographs, the Latin inscriptions, the animal remains.
- A player with knowledge of the occult or The Forgotten might recognize the epithets. Others would simply find it deeply disturbing.
- There is no direct evidence linking the storeroom to Jonathan Oesch, unless the players have already connected him to the case through other means.
