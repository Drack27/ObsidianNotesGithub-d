# Joe Mencha

**Age:** 35
**Residence:** An apartment above [[Joe's Superstore]].
**Occupation:** Owner and operator of Joe's Superstore, the largest retail building in Roselake.

---

## Description

Joe Mencha is a stocky, energetic man with a booming voice and a shopkeeper's instinct for knowing what his customers need before they ask. He runs the biggest store in Roselake — a general-purpose retail operation that stocks everything from canned goods to work boots to livestock feed — and he takes genuine pride in keeping the shelves full for a town that has nowhere else to go.

Joe is practical, friendly, and relentlessly optimistic in the way that small-business owners in dying towns have to be. He knows the demographics are against him. He knows the young people are leaving. He keeps the doors open anyway because the people who are still here need somewhere to buy groceries.

He is one of the more socially connected people in Roselake — most of the town passes through his store at least once a week, which makes him a reliable source of gossip and local news. He doesn't seek out information, but he hears everything.

---

## Relevance

Joe lent Thomas McMullin a cage trap during the Scratching investigation. He is a potential source of information about townspeople's comings and goings, recent purchases, and behavioral changes — the kind of ambient intelligence that comes from running the only real store in a small town.
