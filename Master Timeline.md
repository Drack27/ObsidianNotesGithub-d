### March 15, 1981 — Session 2: Agatha Graves

- **Case Connection:** All cases / [[Anglerfish]]
- **[[Agatha Graves]]** arrives at [[Mount Roselake]] in the morning, responding a day late to the Ghost Hunters Club flyers.
- At the base of the hill, she discovers a patch of flattened grass soaked with dried blood and finds a **large, thin, enameled tooth with dried blood** — physical evidence of the Anglerfish's attack on Thomas McMullin the previous night.
- She hikes to the [[The Old Radio Tower|Old Radio Tower]], enters through the ajar front door, and descends to Thomas McMullin's basement lair.
- She reviews all six case files on Thomas's desk (**The Scratching**, **The Weeping House**, **The Blurred Woman**, **The Missing Missing**, **The Mirror Haunt**, **The Peabody House**) and the full Skinwalkers corkboard, which documents six suspected Anglerfish replacements including Mayor Lance Winnipeg.
- Reading the Weeping House folder, Agatha recognizes **Clara Marsh** as a recent distressed customer at her shop.
- She returns to town and visits the [[Roselake Library]], attempting to identify the tooth using the nature section. After an extended search, she fails — the library has no resources that can identify it.
- The tooth remains in Agatha's possession, unidentified.
- She handles and re-shelves Thomas's folders before leaving, but does not restore them perfectly.

---

### March 16, 1981 — Session 3: Matthew Davis & Agatha Graves

- **Case Connection:** All cases / [[Anglerfish]]
- **[[Matthew Davis]]** visits **[[Agatha Graves]]**'s shop on the second day of his recovery. The two share what they know: both attended the Ghost Club (on different nights), both reviewed Thomas's files.
- Agatha shows Matthew the enameled tooth. He compares it to his puncture wounds — the dimensions match. The tooth came from whatever attacked him.
- They visit **[[Joe's Superstore]]**, where Matthew purchases an **heirloom machete** (intricate leather-and-bead scabbard, ~3–4 feet) for $200. Both observe **Wendy Simons** (an [[Anglerfish]] replacement) at checkout lane 7 — staring blankly, mechanical, unresponsive.
- They visit a local farmer (**Ned**, south of town) to borrow a gun. Ned declines; he suggests the fire department.
- At **[[Roselake Fire Station]]**, they meet **[[Fire Chief Bill]]** (handlebar mustache; was on scene the night of Matthew's attack). Bill mentions his crew searched the base of [[Mount Roselake]] on the morning of March 15 and found nothing. Matthew (appearance, hard success) convinces Bill to accompany them.
- **Daytime investigation at Mount Roselake:** Matthew locates the attack site (hard Archaeology success). He discovers the creature's tracks (hard Tracking success, 2 luck spent): **spherical indentations with a fan-shaped arrangement on one half**, spaced unnaturally far apart — implying extraordinary speed. Matthew fails to persuade Bill to join a nighttime stakeout (persuasion, fail). Bill declines and returns to town.
- **Evening stakeout (~8 PM – 11 PM):** Matthew and Agatha return alone. They investigate the radio station: top floor unchanged; in the basement, Matthew (hard Intelligence success) notices the folders have been disturbed — Agatha confirms this is the result of her own imperfect re-shelving during her Session 2 visit, not a third party. No creature appears during the two-hour vigil. They head home.

### March 17, 1981 — Session 4: Victor Hayes
- **Case Connection:** All cases / [[Anglerfish]]
- **[[Victor Hayes]]** (semi-retired FBI agent) investigates Mount Roselake in the evening. He finds the radio tower basement and takes all six case files (Scratching, Weeping House, Blurred Woman, Missing Missing, Mirror Haunt, Peabody House), The Skinwalkers folder, and three old VHS tapes.
- Investigates Rose Lake and discovers the [[Intrusion Institute Monitoring Station]] hidden along the fence line, along with a nitrate fertilizer runoff ditch.
- Visits [[Joe's Superstore]] to observe and purchases a first aid kit.
- Visits Charlie's Quick Stop / Sam's Video Rentals. Investigating the alley, he spots the [[Anglerfish]] — a vantablack, light-absorbing entity — slinking away with the deflated skin of Thomas McMullin (tweed blazer, khaki pants) draped over it as a lure.
- Dumpster dives behind the quick stop and recovers two gunky VHS tapes, a screwdriver, and a mysterious crud-covered electronic machine.

### March 17, 1981 — Session 5: Matthew Davis & Reverend Detty
- **Case Connection:** All cases / [[Anglerfish]]
- **Same in-game evening as [[Session 4]].** [[Victor Hayes]] was at the [[The Old Radio Tower|Old Radio Tower]] earlier in the evening and took all of Thomas's files before Matthew and Detty arrived. Neither PC knows this.
- **Morning:** [[Matthew Davis]] wakes on the third day of his recovery and regains 1 HP. He drives to [[Joe's Superstore]] and purchases approximately 500 rounds of .45 caliber ammunition for around $100. [[Wendy Simons]] is not at her usual register — a scruffy, stubble-faced man is working the lane in her place. The store is noticeably quieter than on previous visits.
- **Day:** [[Agatha Graves]] does not make the planned rendezvous. Matthew decides to proceed alone to the evening stakeout.
- **Evening (~9 PM):** At the base of [[Mount Roselake]], Matthew meets [[Reverend Detty|Reverend George "Detty" Ndetti]] (introduced). Detty is the pastor of **First Methodist**. He has come up independently, uneasy about the old "Roselake Ghost Hunters Club" flyer (originally posted by [[Thomas McMullin]]) still around town and concerned about his parishioner [[Wendy Simons]]: she has stopped attending church and has been stiff and distant at the store.
- **Old Radio Tower:** The two investigate the station together. The top floor is unchanged. The basement has been **stripped**: no folders on the desk, the plastic crate is empty, the Skinwalkers folder and all of the corkboard's pinned contents are gone. (Canonically: taken by Victor Hayes earlier that same evening.) Disturbed cardboard boxes in the corner contain water-damaged reel-to-reel tapes with smeared, illegible labels. Outside, examination of the rusted generator (extreme success, both investigators) confirms it is non-functional and has been recently tampered with — screws loosened, wrench left on top. Yet the basement lamp is still lit. The power source is unknown. Detty (regular success on Tracking) finds multiple sets of recent human shoeprints around the structure; Matthew pushes his own tracking attempt with archaeological precision and erases the prints in the process.
- **The fall:** The two attempt to climb the radio tower. A gust of wind leans the rusting structure and both fall roughly twenty feet. [[Matthew Davis]] takes **12 damage in a single hit**, dropping him below 0 HP instantly — he suffers a compound fracture of his right arm and a broken spine, and is knocked unconscious. [[Reverend Detty]] fumbles his dexterity roll, takes 6 damage, and breaks his right arm. Detty stabilizes Matthew with an excellent First Aid roll, then (strength with a penalty die, 10 luck points spent) drags him down the hill to the car over roughly thirty minutes.
- **Roselake Fire Station (~10:30–11 PM):** Detty drives one-handed to the [[Roselake Fire Station]]. **Shawna** takes over — on an excellent Medicine roll she resets the compound fracture and realigns Matthew's spine. She warns that his spine was badly out of alignment and that without proper rest he could be permanently paralyzed. Matthew makes his Constitution check against the pain of the reset and does not wake. The on-duty firefighter (young, clean-shaven, early twenties) splints Detty's arm and asks them not to go back up to the radio tower: "We need you around this town, especially now." Shawna scolds Detty for his "wild theories" and for nearly getting Matthew killed.
- **Session ends:** Matthew remains unconscious at 2/11 HP, temporarily paralyzed. Keeper ruling: ~4 weeks of bed rest will return his mobility; if he pushes himself and fails four major-wound recovery rolls in a row, the paralysis becomes permanent. Detty is conscious but badly injured (broken right arm); he will need time to heal, but is not under Matthew's special condition.

### March 18 – April 3, 1981 — Session 6: Matthew Davis
- **Case Connection:** [[The Antimemetic Killer]]
- **Matthew Davis** regains consciousness at the [[Roselake Fire Station]] and spends approximately five days recovering under the care of **Shawna** (the older dispatcher) and **Steven** (a sandy-haired young firefighter, late teens/early twenties). He heals to full HP. Shawna confirms [[Reverend Detty]] is missing — not at his house, not at the church, not at Tony's.
- **March 23:** Matthew is discharged home by fire truck. [[Fire Chief Bill]] and **Leonard** (a lanky firefighter) rig a walker that interfaces with his back brace and bring him home. Supply of MREs from [[Joe's Superstore]] is arranged. Recovery target: **April 13** (reduced to **April 12** with a double hard success in PT). That night: loud knocking at the front door wakes Matthew; he calls out through the window and the knocking stops.
- **~March 24–27:** Bill and Leonard provide daily physical therapy. Progress is slow. Around March 27, Bill fails to appear. The following morning he calls — confused — and arrives with Leonard: **both deny any memory of the discharge, home setup, or PT sessions.** They have no explanation. Bill attributes it to Matthew's spinal injury affecting his memory; declines to involve the sheriff; promises to continue the PT regardless. The real explanation is unresolved — [[Jonathan Oesch]] appears to have been covertly monitoring and caring for Matthew, intending to keep him alive as a future prisoner ("I wanted to keep you around"). PT continues with Bill appearing to normalize the situation.
- **March 29 (Sunday):** [[Reverend Detty]] fails to appear at his own First Methodist church service. The congregation is left without a pastor. He has not been seen since the night of the accident.
- **March 30 – April 2:** The firefighters stop coming. The fire station's phone returns a dead tone. Matthew loses **1 sanity point** as isolation wears on him. On the **night of April 2**, [[Jonathan Oesch]] appears outside the bedroom window — shines a flashlight in Matthew's face, speaks taunting threats from the dark ("no one will remember you... no one can hear you scream"), and leaves.
- **Night of April 3:** Jonathan returns and forces the front door. In the bedroom: combat. Matthew shoots Jonathan (revolver, hits — wounds his arm). Jonathan wrestles the gun away. Matthew smashes Jonathan with the bedside lamp. Jonathan begins **chanting in Latin** — a ritual invocation Matthew recognizes as a language but cannot parse in the chaos. Jonathan pistol-whips Matthew twice (8 damage total), shoots him once (3 damage, grazing), and finally knocks him out with a punch (4 damage). **Matthew goes to 0 HP** and loses consciousness. Jonathan declares: *"No one will remember your name. He'll be just like me."* He is now in possession of Matthew's revolver. Matthew is alive but unconscious and helpless.
- **Session ends:** Matthew is at 0 HP on the floor of his living room. Jonathan Oesch has his gun. Detty remains missing. The fire station is unreachable.

### ~1840s — Founding of Roselake, Nebraska
- Roselake established as an agricultural community on lazily rolling hills in rural Nebraska.
- Only one road in or out: Mule Deer Road (runs north–south).
- The town develops around farming, with a small cluster of civic buildings at its center.

---

### ~1847 — Birth of Margaret Anne Hollowell
- **Case Connection:** *The Case of the Blurred Woman*
- Margaret Anne born to Ezekiel and Martha Hollowell, an early Roselake farming family.
- From childhood, Margaret Anne never fits in with the community.
- She is reclusive, unmarried, and considered "strange" by neighbors.
- Practices folk healing using herbs and poultices — this further alienates her from the churchgoing community.
- Not invited to church socials; gradually omitted from community records.
- Neighbors stop acknowledging her existence entirely.

---

### ~1880 — Both Hollowell Parents Deceased
- **Case Connection:** *The Case of the Blurred Woman*
- With both Ezekiel and Martha dead, Margaret Anne is completely alone.
- Her isolation deepens. The community's deliberate forgetting of her intensifies.

---

### Winter 1889 — Death of Margaret Anne Hollowell
- **Case Connection:** *The Case of the Blurred Woman*
- Margaret Anne dies alone in her house at approximately age 42.
- Her body is not discovered for weeks.
- She is buried in an unmarked plot in the north section of the Old Cemetery.
- No headstone is placed. No entry is made in the church registry. No surviving records of her burial exist.
- The community's erasure of her is complete — she is deliberately, thoroughly forgotten.
- This concentrated, communal act of forgetting creates the supernatural conditions for her later manifestation as the Blurred Woman.
- Her ghost becomes caught in a tug-of-war with The Forgotten entity: she can only manifest through others' fear of being forgotten, but that same force keeps her trapped.

---

### Late 1800s–1960s — The Old Cemetery Ages
- The Old Cemetery (north section) falls into disuse as newer burial plots open.
- Margaret Anne's unmarked grave becomes indistinguishable from the surrounding ground.
- Any oral memory of her existence fades completely from Roselake's collective knowledge.

## Part 2: Mid-Century Events (1920s–1970s)

---

### Early 1900s — Marsh House Built
- **Case Connection:** *The Weeping House* (Red Herring)
- An older house is constructed on the south end of Roselake.
- Plumbing is galvanized steel — standard for the era, but destined to corrode over decades.
- This house will later be home to Reuben and Clara Marsh.

---

### ~1920s–1940s — Peabody Family Prominence
- **Case Connection:** *The Lonely Painting*
- The Peabody family is a large, prominent Roselake family (~12 members).
- Elias Peabody serves as the town pastor.
- The family owns a farmhouse approximately 4 miles southwest of town.

---

### Early 1960s — Reuben & Clara Marsh Join the New Age Movement
- **Case Connection:** *The Weeping House* (Red Herring)
- The Marshes (now in their late 50s as of 1981) embrace new age spirituality.
- Their house fills with tarot cards, crystals, chakra books, and a dedicated seance room.
- This spiritual worldview will later color their interpretation of mundane plumbing problems as demonic activity.

---

### ~1968 — Last Peabody Family Members Leave Roselake
- **Case Connection:** *The Lonely Painting*
- Over the years, the large Peabody family dwindles as members leave town or die.
- By ~1968, only patriarch Elias Peabody remains in the farmhouse.
- Elias lives in profound isolation for the next ~8 years.
- During these years of solitude, Elias creates a large gray landscape painting:
  - Massive cornfield stretching to the horizon
  - Heavy gray cloudy sky
  - A single dead tree far in the distance
- The painting is locked in the attic.
- The concentrated communal loneliness of Roselake — and Elias's personal isolation — allows **The Isolation** entity to supernaturally imbue the painting with power.
- The painting functions like an anglerfish lure: it draws in anyone who lingers near it while feeling lonely, trapping them inside.

---

### ~1976 — Death of Elias Peabody
- **Case Connection:** *The Lonely Painting*
- Elias Peabody dies of natural causes after ~8 years of near-total isolation.
- The Peabody farmhouse is put on the market.
- The supernaturally imbued painting remains locked in the attic, waiting.

## Part 3: The Lonely Painting Vanishings (1976–1981)

---

### ~1976–1977 — Peabody House Family #1: The Jenkins
- **Case Connection:** *The Lonely Painting*
- A young family of 5 moves into the Peabody farmhouse after Elias's death.
- Daughter **Ida Jenkins** feels bullied at school for her old-fashioned name.
- While exploring the house, Ida discovers the painting locked in the attic.
- She becomes obsessed with it — the painting preys on her loneliness and isolation.
- Ida is sucked into the painting.
- The remaining family members, consumed by grief and the lingering loneliness of loss, are drawn in one by one.
- All 5 Jenkins family members vanish.
- The Sheriff investigates. No evidence of foul play is found. Case goes cold.

---

### ~1977–1979 — Peabody House Family #2: The Calvins
- **Case Connection:** *The Lonely Painting*
- A family of 3 moves into the Peabody farmhouse from out of town.
- Son **Isaac Calvin** — described as an edgelord with metal/emo aesthetic — finds the painting and thinks it fits his vibe.
- Isaac is sucked into the painting.
- His parents follow the same pattern of familial grief, drawn in after him.
- All 3 Calvin family members vanish.
- The Sheriff investigates again. Again finds nothing. Second cold case at the same address.

---

### ~1977 — Gus Brennan's Wife Dies
- **Case Connection:** *The Case of the Blurred Woman*
- **Gus Brennan** (70s as of 1981) loses his wife.
- He becomes a semi-reclusive widower living alone on Mill Road.
- A deep, unspoken fear of being forgotten — athazagoraphobia — takes root in him.
- This fear will make him the only repeat witness of the Blurred Woman.

---

### ~Late February 1981 (~4 weeks before campaign start) — Peabody House Family #3: The Jacksons
- **Case Connection:** *The Lonely Painting*
- An artistic family of 4 moves in: **David** (writer), **Grace** (painter), **Emily** (musician), **Sara** (sculptor), plus their dog **Filmore**.
- Unlike previous families, the Jacksons are eccentric and hang the painting in their main living area rather than leaving it in the attic.
- Emily and Sara stop showing up to school.
- Approximately 4 weeks before campaign start (~late February 1981), someone checks on the family.
- The house is empty. All 4 Jacksons are gone.
- Filmore the dog is found half-starved, having survived alone for roughly a week.
- All 4 family members were sucked into the painting.
- The Sheriff investigates for the third time at the same house. Finds nothing for the third time.

### Date Unknown (Estimated Late 1980) — Jonathan Oesch's First Victim: Victor Mann
- **Case Connection:** *The Antimemetic Killer (The Case of the Missing Missing)*
- **Jonathan Oesch** (25 as of 1981): shaggy blonde-brown hair, extremely muscular, soft-spoken. Lives in a small 1950s house on the south side of the Roselake town center. Has always felt rejected by the community; his resentment has curdled into a desire to weaponize fear.
- Jonathan receives insane inspiration from **The Forgotten** entity — a ritual spell called **The Catalyst**.
- **The Catalyst requirements:**
  1. A brain (at least pig intelligence)
  2. A likeness of the target (photo, drawing, or carving)
  3. A circle drawn around the likeness with Latin epithets invoking The Forgotten
  4. The ritual must be left somewhere even the caster forgets (Jonathan uses a bedside clock reminder to visit ~weekly)
- **Target: Victor Mann** — janitor at Roselake Academy.
  - Lived alone; active in community but with no deep personal relationships (perfect victim profile).
  - Appeared in newspaper: Community cleanup day photo, Fall Festival announcement, First Methodist Church directory.
- Jonathan uses a **pig brain** for the ritual. A missing pig from a local farm is reported around the same time (written off as a coyote kill).
- The ritual succeeds: Victor is erased from the memories of everyone who knew him.
- Physical records (newspapers, photos) remain unaffected — they are the only evidence he ever existed.
- **Victor is NOT dead.** He is alive, imprisoned in Jonathan's basement behind a heavily secured door (deadbolts, steel crossbar, padlocks).
- Jonathan regularly visits to psychologically torment him: reminding Victor that no one remembers him, no one is looking, no one cares.
- Jonathan makes no effort to conceal the heavy security on the basement door — he is confident no one will come looking for a person they've forgotten.

---

### Date Unknown (Between Victor and Sally) — Murder of Bill Donahue
- **Case Connection:** *The Antimemetic Killer*
- **Bill Donahue** — a truck driver with a regular route through Roselake. The least social of the regular drivers; didn't hang around Tony's Diner or make small talk.
- Jonathan murders Bill Donahue to harvest his **human brain** for a more powerful Catalyst ritual.
- Bill's body is found on the outskirts of town.
- Only ONE newspaper article covers his death.
- The Sheriff shows NO interest in pursuing an investigation. No follow-up occurs.
- Bill Donahue is the only outright murder victim in the Antimemetic Killer case.
- His death timeline aligns with the disappearances of Victor Mann and Sally Oesch.

---

### Estimated Early–Mid February 1981 (~3–5 weeks before campaign) — Jonathan's Second Erasure: Sally Oesch
- **Case Connection:** *The Antimemetic Killer*
- **Sally Oesch** — Jonathan's own sister. Lived with Jonathan. Fairly active in community (church volunteer, charity drives) but had no close personal bonds.
- Jonathan uses **Bill Donahue's human brain** for this Catalyst ritual — far more powerful than the pig brain used on Victor.
- The human-brain-powered spell thoroughly erases Sally from all social memory, including **family-level bonds** — a depth the pig brain could not achieve.
- Sally appears in newspaper archives: Church directory, charity drive article.
- **Sally is NOT dead.** She is alive in Jonathan's basement alongside Victor.
- She is subjected to the same psychological torment.
