# The Old Cemetery

**Type:** Location
**Location:** On the outskirts of Roselake, east of town past the church.
**Case Connection:** [[The Case of the Blurred Woman]]

---

## Description

The Old Cemetery is Roselake's original burial ground, dating back to the town's founding in the 1840s. It sits on a gentle rise east of town, past the First Methodist Church, bordered by a low stone wall that has crumbled in several places. A wrought-iron gate marks the entrance, though it hasn't been properly latched in years.

The cemetery is still technically in use — newer burials occupy the south end, with neat rows of granite headstones dating from the mid-twentieth century onward. But the north end, where the oldest graves are, is another matter entirely.

## The Old Section

The northern portion of the cemetery contains graves from the 1840s through the 1880s. Many of the headstones are weathered to the point of illegibility — names and dates eroded by a century and a half of Nebraska weather. Some graves are marked only by fieldstones with no inscription at all. Several wooden markers have rotted away entirely, leaving shallow depressions in the earth where plots once were.

This section is overgrown. The grass is tall, the ground uneven, and several headstones have tilted or fallen. Nobody tends to these graves. Nobody visits them. The people buried here have been forgotten — not supernaturally, but in the ordinary, inevitable way that time erases the dead.

## Connection to The Blurred Woman

The Blurred Woman — the ghost who haunts Roselake homes with her unfocusable face — was a real person who lived in the Roselake area in the mid-to-late 1800s. If she was buried at all, she was buried here, in the old section of this cemetery.

Finding her grave is one of the key steps to identifying her and potentially laying her to rest. The challenge is that many graves in the old section are unmarked, illegible, or missing entirely. Players would need to cross-reference old county records, church registries, and land deeds with the physical layout of the cemetery to identify which grave — if any — belongs to her.

The county records office and the [[Roselake Library]] may contain the oldest surviving records of burials. The church may have its own historical registry. But records from the 1840s-1880s in a small Nebraska town are sparse, incomplete, and often contradictory.

## Atmosphere

The Old Cemetery is not supernaturally active — not in the way the Marsh house or the Wakefield house is. But it is profoundly, deeply quiet. The kind of quiet that makes you aware of your own breathing. The old section, in particular, has an atmosphere of absolute stillness — as if even the wind avoids it.

On nights when The Forgotten's influence is strong, the cemetery feels heavier. Names on headstones seem harder to read. The feeling of being surrounded by people that no one remembers is palpable and oppressive.

## Relevance to Players

- Players investigating the Blurred Woman case will likely need to visit the Old Cemetery to search for her grave.
- Old county burial records (available at [[Castle Roselake]] or the [[Roselake Library]]) may help narrow down which plots belong to which era and which individuals.
- The cemetery itself is not dangerous, but spending extended time in the old section — particularly at night — may amplify feelings of loneliness, insignificance, and the fear of being forgotten. These are ambient effects of The Forgotten's presence, not a direct threat.
