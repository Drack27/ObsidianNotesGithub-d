# Sam Reardon

Operator of **Sam's Video Rentals**, the corrugated metal shack attached to [[The Convenience and Video Rental Store|Charlie's Quick Stop]].

## Description

Sam Reardon is young, blonde, and operates at a frequency most people don't quite share. He has the wild, voluminous hair and frenetic energy of a man who treats every conversation as an emergency and every piece of broken hardware as a personal challenge. He talks fast, gestures a lot, and is constitutionally incapable of giving a short answer about anything related to film or electronics. He is incongruous in rural Nebraska and seems entirely unbothered by this.

His video rental shop reflects his personality: dimly lit, psychedelic patterning on the carpet, movie posters covering every inch of wall (*The Thing*, *Alien*, *Jaws*). It is a genuine enthusiast's shrine.

## Role in Roselake

Sam is the person to see for anything involving consumer electronics, VHS repair, or an impassioned forty-minute argument about *Apocalypse Now*. He is technically capable and genuinely enjoys working on equipment — the repair room in the back is his natural habitat.

He is not a source of town gossip in the way Tony Manitole is. He notices things — more than he lets on — but his instinct is to let unusual things go rather than press on them.

## Investigator Interactions

**March 18 – April 3, 1981 (between sessions):** [[Victor Hayes]] and [[Marcus Vale]] bring Sam the crud-covered mystery machine Victor recovered from the dumpster behind the Quick Stop. Sam identifies it as a broken VHS player, sources the parts needed to fix it, and helps with the repair. Victor and Marcus screen the two dumpster tapes; both contain only commercial films.

**[[Session 7]] — April 4, 1981 (morning):** Victor and Marcus visit. Victor hands Sam a reel-to-reel tape (dated December 29, 1950) to examine. Sam is genuinely surprised — it's professional broadcast equipment, strange to find in a private collection. He asks where Victor got it; Victor's attempts to explain fail. Sam notices but lets it go. His verdict: he has no playback equipment for reel-to-reel, and as far as he knows, nothing in Roselake does either. He suggests that if Victor can find a reel-to-reel player somewhere — maybe in someone's attic — Sam can help set it up.
