# PART ONE — Thomas's File

_What Thomas McMullin has gathered: newspaper clippings, county records, and his own notes. This is what players/readers would encounter if they looked through his materials._

## Thomas's Theory

Thomas has identified three people who seem to have vanished from Roselake — not just physically, but from _memory._ Nobody in town remembers them. The Sheriff never investigated. It's as if they were never here. The only reason Thomas knows about them at all is because he found their names in old issues of the Roselake Register, where they appeared in community event coverage, church bulletins, and local interest pieces.

Thomas suspects some kind of government cover-up or conspiracy — possibly a relocation program, witness protection gone wrong, or something more sinister. He doesn't have a solid theory yet, just the gut certainty that three real people have been erased and no one cares.

## The Three Missing

**Victor Mann** — Janitor at Roselake School. Thomas found multiple newspaper mentions: Victor helped organize the school's fall festival, was photographed at a community cleanup day, appeared in a church directory. He was a visible, if quiet, member of the community. He lived alone. At some point, all record of his daily life just _stops._ His house went up for sale. Nobody at the school remembers employing him. Thomas asked around — blank stares.

**Bill Donahue** — One of the regular truck drivers who passed through Roselake on a recurring route. The least social of the drivers who frequented the town, but known enough to appear in a brief Register mention. His body was found on the outskirts of town around the same time that traces of Victor and Sally started disappearing from public life. The Sheriff's investigation into his death was cursory at best. Thomas considers this deeply suspicious and possibly connected to the other two disappearances.

**Sally Oesch** — The most recent to vanish. Sally lived with her brother Jonathan and was a fairly active community member — church events, volunteer work — though she didn't seem to have close personal relationships. Thomas found her name in several Register articles. Now, no one in town acknowledges she existed. Her brother Jonathan still lives in the same house. Thomas hasn't confronted him yet.

## Thomas's Notes

Thomas finds it profoundly alarming that no one remembers these people. Not "they moved away and people forgot" — _nobody recognizes their names or faces._ Community members who were photographed _standing next to them_ at events have no memory of them.

He's leaning toward government conspiracy theories — experimental amnesia programs, chemical exposure, something federal — but can't make it fit neatly. Bill Donahue's death is the anchor point that connects the timeline, and the fact that the Sheriff showed no interest in pursuing it further fuels Thomas's paranoia.

# PART TWO — What's Actually Happening

_The full truth. Thomas does not have access to any of this information._

## Jonathan Oesch

Jonathan Oesch has always felt rejected by his community. That rejection curdled into deep resentment. He began to see himself as a lone raider — even as he feared isolation and being forgotten, he wanted to _weaponize_ that fear against the rest of Roselake. He wanted them to feel everything he had felt.

Jonathan is not a killer (with one exception). He is something far worse. He is a major driver of **the Fear of the Forgotten** — the Lonely's cousin, the thing that erases you from the minds of everyone who ever knew you.

## The Catalyst

Jonathan uses a ritual spell called **The Catalyst**, knowledge of which was granted to him through insane inspiration channeled from **The Forgotten.** The spell works as follows:

**Requirements:**

- The brain of an animal at least as intelligent as a pig. The more powerful the brain (pig = weak, human = strong), the more thoroughly the spell can overcome strong social bonds.
- A likeness of the target — a photograph, carving, drawing, or any representational image.
- A circle drawn around the likeness, inscribed with epithets calling upon The Forgotten. Examples: _"It That Erases All That Is Loved," "Great Non-Socializer,"_ etc. Jonathan writes these in Latin — not because that's a requirement, but because it feels right to him.
- The completed ritual must then be left somewhere that even the caster forgets about it. (Jonathan sets reminders for himself.)

**Effects:**

Once the Catalyst takes effect, it erases the target from the memories of everyone who knew them. All associates, friends, and family lose their memories of the target. Existing memories are not simply suppressed — the target is _painted out,_ as if photoshopped from the recollections of everyone who ever interacted with them.

The strength of the brain determines how deeply the spell can cut through strong bonds. A pig brain can erase casual acquaintances and coworkers. A human brain can sever family ties and deep friendships.

## What Jonathan Does After

Once the spell takes effect and no one remembers the target, Jonathan kidnaps them and locks them in his basement. He keeps them alive. He visits them regularly and reminds them, over and over, that no one is thinking about them. No one is looking for them. No one remembers they exist.

## The Victims (In Order)

**1. Victor Mann** — Janitor at Roselake School. Jonathan's first victim. Victor lived alone and was active in the community but had no close personal relationships — making him an ideal target. The Catalyst was performed using an animal brain (likely a pig). The spell thoroughly erased Victor from the town's collective memory. Victor is currently alive in Jonathan's basement.

**2. Bill Donahue** — A truck driver who regularly passed through Roselake. The least social of the regular drivers. Bill is Jonathan's only murder victim — he was killed so Jonathan could harvest his brain for use in a more powerful casting of the Catalyst. Bill's body was found on the outskirts of town, but the Sheriff's investigation went nowhere, in part because no one felt strongly connected to Bill and the spell's ambient influence may have further dulled interest.

**3. Sally Oesch** — Jonathan's own sister. His most recent victim. Sally lived with Jonathan and was fairly active in the community, but like Victor, she lacked close personal bonds. Jonathan used Bill Donahue's human brain to power the Catalyst for Sally — a much stronger casting, ensuring even her community-level connections were severed completely. Sally is currently alive in Jonathan's basement.

## Why It Works So Well

Victor and Sally were both socially active but lacked _deep_ personal relationships. They showed up to events, volunteered, attended church — but nobody was truly close to them. This made the Catalyst devastating: there were no strong emotional bonds to resist the spell's erasure. The town simply... forgot.

The only traces left are in print — old newspaper articles, church bulletins, public records — which is how Thomas stumbled onto the case in the first place. The Catalyst erases memory, not paper.
