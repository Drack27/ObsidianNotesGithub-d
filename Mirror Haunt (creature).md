# Mirror Haunt (Creature)

**Type:** Entity Spawn
**Origin:** [[The Secondary Entities|The Eye]]
**Associated Fear:** The fear of being watched — scopophobia, the intimate violation of never being alone.
**Case Connection:** [[Mirror Haunt]]

---

## Overview

A Mirror Haunt is a spawn of **The Eye** — a Secondary Entity born from a mingling of **The Uncanny** (the questioning of reality) and **The Inevitable** (the crushing weight of dread). While The Eye does not have a major presence in Roselake itself, it has a heavy presence among staff members of the nearby **Intrusion Institute**, who are accustomed to the world of espionage and its dangers. The Mirror Haunt is a creature born from that concentrated fear of surveillance, observation, and violation of privacy.

Ironically, the Mirror Haunt and entities associated with The Eye are among the **most benign** of the Secondary Entities and their spawn. The Eye is largely opposed to the existence of many of the other Entities — it is hard to be afraid of what has something else watching over it. It is hard to be afraid of the dark when you can see in it. The Eye's watchfulness, while terrifying to its individual targets, functions as a kind of inadvertent check on other fears.

---

## Appearance

A nondescript, extremely skinny, gaunt humanoid shape wearing a plain bluish off-white nightgown. Its most distinctive feature is its head: a single giant round orb with no features except a pair of lips that span almost the entirety of the front of the head. When the mouth is closed, the head is a smooth, featureless sphere with only the seam of the lips visible.

When the mouth opens, it reveals teeth framing a **single giant eyeball** that occupies the entirety of the interior of the head. The veins of the eye are visible shifting through the skin — the creature has no skull. The skin of the head is essentially a thin membrane stretched over the eye.

**The feeling of being watched only begins when the mouth is open.** The creature can be present in a mirror with its mouth closed and the victim will feel nothing. The watching is the point.

---

## Behavior & Escalation

The Mirror Haunt follows a deliberate, slow escalation pattern. **The entire reason for the slow ramp-up is that the creature knows it is fragile** — it is very much not immortal and can be killed easily. It takes its time to ensure it is deeply embedded in the target's psyche before revealing itself fully.

### Stage 1 — Presence
The victim begins to feel watched when near reflective surfaces. Nothing is visible. Just the prickling sensation of being observed. This can last days or weeks.

### Stage 2 — Glimpses
The victim begins to catch brief, peripheral glimpses of something in mirrors — a shape that shouldn't be there, a flicker of movement. When they look directly, nothing. This stage erodes sleep and trust in one's own perception.

### Stage 3 — Manifestation
The Mirror Haunt becomes fully visible in reflective surfaces. It stands behind or beside the victim in the reflection, mouth open, eye staring. It does not appear in the physical space — only in the reflection. The victim can see it watching them, but cannot interact with it or escape its gaze except by removing or covering all reflective surfaces.

### Stage 4 — Emergence
**Nobody currently knows this is possible**, as it has never happened before. If the escalation is allowed to continue unchecked for another several weeks beyond Stage 3, the Mirror Haunt will **step out of a reflection and into the physical world.** Once in the physical world, it will follow the target everywhere, never giving them a moment of peace — an inescapable, omnipresent watcher.

At this stage, the Mirror Haunt will make every effort to **keep itself concealed from everyone except the person it has dedicated itself to watching.** It is aware that it is fragile and can be killed easily by others; its survival depends on remaining invisible to everyone but its chosen target.

If, at Stage 4, the target of the haunting **fully gives in to their fear** — the equivalent of sanity hitting zero in Call of Cthulhu's rules — the manifested Mirror Haunt and the target will **merge** into a singular, powerful **Disciple of The Eye**, with the capability to someday become an Avatar of The Eye.

---

## Target Selection

The Mirror Haunt that currently inhabits Roselake initially considered latching onto a staff member of the **Intrusion Institute** — the nearby covert paranormal monitoring station — as The Eye's presence is strongest among people accustomed to espionage and surveillance. However, upon observing the Institute's tools and capabilities, the creature decided against it. The Institute's operatives are too well-equipped, too aware, and too dangerous.

Instead, it chose **[[Mirror Haunt|Edith Zelenskyy]]** — one of the most paranoid people in town. Edith's deep-seated fear of losing her home (rooted in her years of homelessness in Omaha) has left her hypervigilant, anxious, and acutely sensitive to the feeling of being watched. She was the perfect target: isolated, afraid, and unlikely to seek help.

---

## Vulnerabilities

The Mirror Haunt is **fragile.** It is not immortal. It can be killed, and killed relatively easily — if it can be reached. The challenge is that, in Stages 1-3, it exists only within reflective surfaces and cannot be physically interacted with. In Stage 4, it exists in the physical world but actively conceals itself from everyone except its target.

Potential avenues for dealing with a Mirror Haunt:
- Destroying all reflective surfaces denies it a home but does not kill it — it will simply wait.
- Trapping it in a mirror and destroying the mirror may work, but has never been tested.
- If it has emerged into the physical world (Stage 4), it can be physically killed, but only if it can be located — which requires either the target's cooperation or extraordinary perception.
- Severing the target's fear — genuinely alleviating their sense of being watched — may cause the Mirror Haunt to lose its anchor and dissipate. This is theoretical.
