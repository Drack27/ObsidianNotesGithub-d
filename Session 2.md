# Session 2

**Date (in-game):** March 15, 1981, morning through afternoon
**Player Character:** Agatha Graves
**Player:** Abbie

---

## Events

### Arrival at Mount Roselake

[[Agatha Graves]] arrived at the base of [[Mount Roselake]] in the morning, driving her hearse out to the dirt lot at the foot of the hill. She had seen the flyers advertising the "Roselake Ghost Hunters Club" around town and had come to investigate — a day late, as the first meeting had been the previous evening.

Before she even began hiking, Agatha noticed something odd: a large patch of flattened and smashed grass off to one side of the path. Closer examination (an extreme success on Spot Hidden) revealed dark red dried blood coating much of the area — and, barely visible between blades of grass, a large, thin, enameled tooth with dried blood on it.

Agatha pocketed the tooth. The evidence at the base of the hill — the flattened grass, the blood, the shed tooth — is the aftermath of the [[Anglerfish]]'s attack on [[Thomas McMullin]] the previous night.

### The Old Radio Tower

Agatha hiked to the top of the hill and reached the [[The Old Radio Tower|Old Radio Tower]]: a rusted, creaking radio antenna and a blocky concrete structure at its base, partially built into the hillside. She noted a front door (slightly ajar), a smashed window, a side door leading to a basement, and a rusted generator against the wall.

She peered in through the front door (a pushed Spot Hidden roll, hard success) and saw the disused interior: racks of rusted radio equipment, cobwebs, a cracked "On Air" sign hanging by one wire, and metal stairs leading down to the basement. Light was coming from below.

Agatha descended.

### The Basement — Thomas's Lair

The basement was cleaner than the floor above — messy in an organized, investigative way rather than simple decay. Agatha found:

- A wooden desk with a swivel chair pushed back, an old lamp illuminating a spread of manila folders, a typewriter, and a plastic crate full of empty folders.
- A large 4×3-foot corkboard on the east wall, covered in photographs, index cards, and red yarn connecting them — a full conspiracy board. A manila folder labeled **The Case of the Skinwalkers** rested on the corkboard's ledge.
- Half-rotted cardboard boxes in one corner containing old reel-to-reel recording tapes, rusted spare parts (presumably for the radio equipment upstairs), and crumbling documents too degraded to read.

### The Case Files

Six manila folders were laid out on the desk, each labeled in black marker. Agatha reviewed all of them:

**The Scratching (Hutchins House)** — The thickest folder, held shut with two rubber bands and a binder clip. Inside: multiple pages of handwritten notes, photographs, hand-drawn trap placement diagrams, a stakeout log, and a timeline of "entity movements" plotted on graph paper.

**The Weeping House** — A thinner folder. Notes, two photographs, and a hand-drawn floor plan. Reading the name Clara Marsh triggered recognition: Agatha knows Clara as a customer at her shop. Clara came in recently looking haggard — bags under her eyes, a wild and twitchy manner — and bought crosses and rosaries. Agatha had no Catholic church stock before, but Clara needed them.

**The Case of the Blurred Woman** — A folder of average thickness. Interview notes on index cards (left pocket), summary notes on lined paper (right), and a single photograph paperclipped to the inside cover.

**The Missing Missing** — One of the thicker folders. Newspaper clippings from the *Roselake Register* annotated with Thomas's handwritten margin notes, a loose hand-drawn timeline sketched on the back of a Tony's Diner placemat, and a sticky note.

**The Mirror Haunt** — A thinner folder. The tab label has a question mark added later in a different pen. Inside: photographs and notes.

**The Peabody House** — Thicker, with a notation beneath the label reading *"three families, twelve people, zero answers."* Photographs of the farmhouse paperclipped inside the front cover, a *Roselake Register* clipping from roughly three and a half weeks prior, and additional documents. Agatha recalled having heard about this case — the sheriff made an announcement at church, and she may have read about it in the paper.

### The Corkboard — The Case of the Skinwalkers

The east-wall corkboard was dominated by a central index card reading **"The Skinwalkers"** in heavy black marker, with red yarn radiating out to six clusters of photographs and index cards:

- **A teenage girl** with long dark hair and a denim jacket, photographed candidly outside Sam's Quick Stop — and alongside it, a cleaner yearbook photo of the same girl smiling. Index cards in blue ink.
- **A woman at Joe's Superstore**, photographed through the window from outside. She is behind the cash register, mid-scan, staring ahead with a flat, expressionless look — clinical, blank. Index cards attached.
- **An older couple** leaving the First Methodist Church, mouths closed, eyes forward, expressions hollow. A second photo from a church newsletter shows the same couple smiling warmly at a potluck. The contrast is marked.
- **A farmer** in dirty overalls and a sun-bleached cap, standing by a fence post on farmland. He's caught mid-turn, posture slightly off, leaning to one side.
- **A dog** — a medium-sized hound mix — lying stiff-legged on a porch with a dull, glassy expression. A second photo shows the same dog mid-play in happier times.
- **A man in a rumpled button-down shirt**, outside [[Castle Roselake]], mid-gesture and apparently shouting at someone off camera. A second photo from a local newspaper shows him shaking hands at a ribbon cutting, smiling warmly. Index cards in a more frantic hand than the others.

Agatha recognized the last subject: Mayor Lance Winnipeg. She has heard whispers through her shop about the Mayor acting strangely lately. Thomas's notes on this cluster are the most agitated on the board.

The manila folder on the corkboard's ledge held a newspaper clipping, a small note, and a single sheet of lined notebook paper.

### Return to Town — The Library

Agatha drove back into Roselake and went to [[Roselake Library|the library]] at [[Roselake Academy]] to try to identify the tooth she had found.

At the library desk she was greeted by a young bookish woman — slight frame, 20s, glasses on a chain — who recognized her immediately: *"Ms. Graves, pleasure to see you around."* Agatha is apparently a local celebrity; a handful of curious kindergartners followed her inside and had to be shooed out.

Agatha asked for books on animal teeth. The librarian directed her to the nature section in the back. There she found a pale, waif-like girl of about ten — clutching a book about lions open to a graphic photo of a freshly killed antelope — who stared at Agatha, then bolted.

Agatha searched the section for hours (failed Library Use roll, pushed to a bare success at exactly her skill value). She worked through books on predators, pack animals, and wolves. She found nothing that matched the tooth. The Roselake Library has no resources that can identify it.

Session ended with Agatha leaving the library in the afternoon, tooth still unidentified.

---

## Significance

This session introduced [[Agatha Graves]] as the second active player character. She now has in her possession a large, unidentified enameled tooth with dried blood — physical evidence of the [[Anglerfish]]'s attack, though she does not yet know what creature it came from.

Agatha reviewed the full contents of [[Thomas McMullin]]'s case files and corkboard, giving her an overview of all seven active cases. Her prior knowledge of [[The Marsh Couple|Clara Marsh]] as a customer establishes a personal connection to the Weeping House case. Her awareness of rumors about the Mayor links her to the Skinwalkers thread.

The tooth cannot be identified with the library's current resources. Further investigation will require either outside expertise or a different approach.
